# Writing — full guidelines — overview
- **Define your app's voice.** Consider your audience to determine the appropriate vocabulary. What terminology is familiar to users of your application? How should users feel when interacting with the app? For instance, a banking application might use language that conveys stability and trust, whereas a game's language should evoke excitement and enjoyment. Compile a list of common terms and reference it to maintain linguistic consistency. A consistent voice, combined with language that reflects your app's core values, contributes to a cohesive user experience.
- **Align tone with context.** After establishing your app's voice, vary the tone according to the specific situation. Think about what users are doing while using your app—both in the real world and within the application. Are they completing a fitness goal? Or are they attempting a payment and encountering an error? Situational factors influence both the language used and how that text is displayed on screen.

Compare the tone in these two Apple Watch examples. In the first, the tone is direct and straightforward, matching the gravity of the situation. In the second, the tone is lighthearted and congratulatory.

- **Maintain clarity.** Select words that are easily understood and accurately convey the intended message. Scrutinize each word to ensure its necessity. If you can achieve your goal with fewer words, do so. When in doubt, read your writing aloud.
- **Write for all users.** To ensure maximum utility across diverse users, your app must communicate effectively with the broadest possible audience. Employ simple, plain language and write with accessibility and localization in mind, avoiding technical jargon and gendered terms. For guidance, consult [Writing inclusively](https://help.apple.com/applestyleguide/#/apdcb2a65d68) and [VoiceOver](voiceover.md); for developer instructions, refer to [Localization](apple:xcode/localization).
