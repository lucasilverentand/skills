# Order tracking — overview
When you enable order tracking, Wallet displays details regarding an order a customer placed through your application or website. This information updates whenever the order status changes. In iOS 17 and later, you can enable users to begin tracking their orders directly from your platform and offer additional ways for them to add the order to Wallet.

Wallet provides a dashboard that tracks both active and completed orders. Users can select an order to view specific details, such as the items purchased and fulfillment information related to shipping or pickup.

The [Wallet Orders](apple:WalletOrders) schema defines the properties you must use to supply order data, including product descriptions, current order status, contact information, and shipping/pickup specifics like estimated arrival dates, addresses, tracking numbers, and pickup instructions. Wallet presents the data you provide through consistent interfaces defined by the system. To ensure users quickly and conveniently find the information they need, supply as much detail as possible using properties that align with your existing order workflows.

- **Make it easy for people to add an order to Wallet.** For instance, when a customer finalizes an Apple Pay transaction on your site or app, utilize [PKPaymentOrderDetails](apple:PassKit/PKPaymentOrderDetails) (app) or [ApplePayPaymentOrderDetails](apple:ApplePayontheWeb/ApplePayPaymentOrderDetails) (web) to automatically add the order to Wallet. In iOS 17 and later, you can use [AddOrderToWalletButton](apple:FinanceKitUI/AddOrderToWalletButton) to display the system-provided Track with Apple Wallet button in relevant locations within your app or website—such as on order confirmation, status, or tracking pages—or in customer emails. If a user has already added the order to Wallet, attempting to add it again will open Wallet and display the existing order.
- **Make information about an order available immediately after people place it.** Customers require confirmation that their order has been received, even if payment, processing, or fulfillment are still pending. If you will not have complete details until a later time, provide the data available at the moment of ordering and include a status [description](apple:walletorders/order) such as, “Check back later for full order details.”
- **Provide fulfillment information as soon as it’s available, and keep the status up to date.** When you supply fulfillment data or modify an order's status, the system updates the order information and can automatically notify customers. The system uses the fulfillment status you report to update the order’s current status to a value like Order Placed, Processing, Ready for Pickup, Picked Up, Out for Delivery, Delivered, or — if an issue arises — Issue or Canceled. For guidance on describing a status, refer to [Displaying order and fulfillment details](#Displaying-order-and-fulfillment-details).
- **Supply a high-resolution logo image that uses a nontransparent background.** The system displays your logo in both the dashboard and detail views, so it is important that users can instantly recognize it regardless of size. Create a logo image measuring 300x300 pixels using either PNG or JPEG format. To ensure your logo renders correctly, use a background that is nontransparent. For developer guidance, see [logo](apple:walletorders/merchant).
- **Supply distinct, high-resolution product images that use nontransparent backgrounds.** The system displays a product's image—along with the descriptive information you provide—in detail views, the order dashboard, and notifications for an order or fulfillment. When creating a product image, use a clear depiction against a solid, nontransparent background. Presenting a product in a "lifestyle" setting or against a busy backdrop may make it difficult to distinguish at smaller sizes. For each product, create an image measuring 300x300 pixels in PNG or JPEG format.
- **In general, keep text brief.** Users appreciate being able to read information quickly, and the system has limitations on lengthy text.
- **Use clear, approachable language, and localize the text you provide.** You must ensure that all your customers can read the information in an order. Additionally, verify that the price displayed matches the final price confirmed by the customer.
