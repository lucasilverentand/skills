# Designing for iPadOS
iPad is valued for its power, portability, and versatility, enabling users to consume media, play games, execute complex productivity tasks, and bring their creative visions to life

When beginning the design of an app or game for iPad, it is essential to first grasp the fundamental device characteristics and interaction patterns that define the iPadOS experience. Basing your design choices on these characteristics and patterns will help you create an application or game that resonates with iPad users.

- **Display.** The iPad features a large, high-resolution screen.
- **Ergonomics.** Users often hold the iPad while using it, but they may also rest it on a surface or use a stand. The viewing distance can vary depending on how the device is positioned, though users typically interact with it from within about 3 feet.
- **Inputs.** Interaction with the iPad can occur through Multi-Touch [gestures](gestures.md), [virtual keyboards](virtual-keyboards.md), a connected [keyboard](keyboards.md) or [pointing device](pointing-devices.md), [Apple Pencil](apple-pencil-and-scribble.md), or [voice](siri.md). Users frequently combine these different input methods.
- **App interactions.** User activity ranges from quick, brief actions to extended sessions spent immersed in games, media consumption, content creation, or productivity workflows. Users often have multiple applications running concurrently and appreciate the ability to view several apps simultaneously and utilize inter-app features like drag and drop.
- **System features.** iPadOS includes several features that allow users to interact with the system and their applications in ways that are both consistent and familiar.
- [Multitasking](multitasking.md)
- [Widgets](widgets.md)
- [Drag and drop](drag-and-drop.md)

### Best practices
To ensure your experience feels native to iPadOS, prioritize integrating platform and device capabilities using the following approaches:

- Leverage the large display to foreground critical content, minimizing full-screen transitions and modal interfaces. Place on-screen controls within easy reach but without being intrusive.
- Utilize viewing distance and input mode to dictate the scale and density of displayed content.
- Support Multi-Touch gestures, physical keyboards, trackpads, or Apple Pencil, and consider unique interactions that combine multiple input methods.
- Adapt fluidly to appearance variations—including device orientation, multitasking modes, Dark Mode, and Dynamic Type—and transition effortlessly to macOS environments, allowing users to select their preferred configurations.
