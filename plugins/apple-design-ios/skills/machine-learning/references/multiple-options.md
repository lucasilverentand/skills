# Multiple options
Depending on your feature's design, it may be most effective to present a single result or multiple options from which users can select. Offering several choices enhances the user's sense of control and helps align your model's predictions with actual user needs. Furthermore, multiple options can manage users' expectations regarding the types of results your application generates.

You might present multiple choices in these scenarios:

- Suggested options: A proactive feature that proposes content based on the user's prior interactions. For instance, Apple Music's "For You" recommendations.
- Requested options: A reactive feature that suggests potential subsequent actions based on recent user activity. For example, Quick Type suggestions.
- Corrections: Actions users take to rectify errors made by your app while acting on their behalf. For example, the Photos Auto-Crop feature.
- **Prioritize diverse options.** Whenever feasible, balance response accuracy with the variety of multiple choices. For example, Apple Maps typically offers more than one route to a destination, such as options avoiding tolls, scenic routes, or those utilizing highways. Presenting varied choices allows users to select their preference and may introduce them to items of interest they hadn't considered.
- **Generally, avoid overwhelming users with too many options.** Since users must evaluate each choice before committing, a larger number of options increases cognitive load. If possible, display all choices on one screen to prevent users from having to scroll extensively to find the correct one.
- **Present the most probable option first.** If you understand how your [confidence](#Confidence) values relate to result quality, you can use them to rank the options. You may also incorporate contextual data, such as the time of day or current location, to help determine the most likely choice. If it fits your application's context, default to the first option so users can quickly achieve their goals without reviewing every possibility.
- **Ensure options are easily distinguishable and selectable.** For example, in a routing application, users often need to make route decisions rapidly to prevent going astray. When options appear similar, assist users in differentiating them by providing a brief description of each and carefully highlighting the differences. If there are too many options to fit in one view, such as with content recommendations, consider grouping choices into categories that users can quickly scan.
- **Learn from selections when appropriate.** Users provide you with [implicit](#Implicit-feedback) every time they make a choice. If it does not negatively impact the user experience, utilize this feedback to refine your options and increase the likelihood that you present the most probable option first. Generally, continuing to provide incorrect results is likely to diminish users' trust in your app's predictive quality.
