# Implicit feedback
Implicit feedback consists of data generated through how users interact with your application features. Unlike the specific responses gathered from [explicit](#Explicit-feedback), implicit feedback provides a broad spectrum of information regarding user habits and preferences. While incorporating this type of data is not mandatory for developing a superior machine learning application, it can nonetheless assist in enhancing the user experience without requiring users to perform extra actions.

- **Always secure people’s information.** Because implicit feedback may collect potentially sensitive user data, you must maintain rigorous controls regarding user privacy.
- **Help people control their information.** As a developer, you understand that greater insight into user behavior—both within your app and across other applications—allows you to enhance the experience your app provides. Although most users grasp the benefits of making their data available across multiple apps, they may still be surprised when actions taken in one app influence experiences in another. Furthermore, users might assume that apps are sharing their private data, which can erode trust. Therefore, it is vital to inform users how your app collects and shares their information and to provide mechanisms allowing them to restrict the flow of that data.
- **Don’t let implicit feedback decrease people’s opportunities to explore.** Implicit feedback tends to reinforce established user behavior, which can yield short-term improvements but may degrade the experience over time. For instance, suggesting content that perfectly matches current interests might seem beneficial, but it discourages users from discovering novel subjects.
- **When possible, use multiple feedback signals to improve suggestions and mitigate mistakes.** Implicit feedback is indirect, making it challenging to accurately determine a user’s true intent from the gathered data. For example, if a user views a photo, shares it in a message, and adds it to a shared album, this does not automatically signify positive sentiment toward the photo. By incorporating implicit feedback from different sources and interactions, you can help prevent misinterpreting user intentions.
- **Consider withholding private or sensitive suggestions.** Users frequently share accounts and devices, or transition from personal use to communal use. If your app receives implicit feedback concerning private or sensitive subjects, refrain from offering recommendations based on that data.
- **Prioritize recent feedback.** Since user tastes are subject to frequent shifts, base your recommendations on the most current implicit feedback. For example, Face ID prioritizes recent facial input because it is the most likely representation of the user's current appearance. If recent feedback is unavailable, you may rely on historical data.
- **Use feedback to update predictions on a cadence that matches the person’s mental model of the feature.** For instance, users expect typing suggestions to update instantly as they type. Conversely, providing continuously updated song recommendations can make it difficult for users to consider individual tracks and may cause them to feel rushed or overwhelmed.
- **Be prepared for changes in implicit feedback when you make changes to your app’s UI.** Even minor adjustments to the user interface can result in noticeable shifts in the volume and type of implicit feedback. For example, relocating a button affects how users interact with it, even if the underlying function of that action remains unchanged. You must account for such changes when interpreting the implicit feedback received from in-app interactions.
- **Beware of confirmation bias.** Implicit feedback is limited by what users can actually see and do within your app and other apps—it rarely offers insight into potential new activities they might enjoy. Do not base your results solely on implicit feedback.
