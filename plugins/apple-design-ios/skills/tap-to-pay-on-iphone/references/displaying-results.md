# Displaying results
Customers complete payments by bringing a contactless card or digital wallet near the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen within your application. Following a successful tap (and subsequent successful PIN entry, if needed), Tap to Pay on iPhone displays a checkmark and provides your app with an object containing the encrypted payment details that you transmit to your PSP for processing. If a tap fails, Tap to Pay on iPhone presents an error screen. Your application is responsible for showing the transaction outcomes after a successful tap or offering alternative payment methods following an unsuccessful attempt.

- **Initiate transaction processing as quickly as possible.** The system offers an API that allows you to request the result of a successful tap before the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen finishes animating the checkmark indicating completion. For developer guidance, refer to [returnReadResultImmediately](apple:ProximityReader/PaymentCardReader/Options-swift.struct/returnReadResultImmediately).
- **Show a progress indicator while payment is being authorized before displaying your transaction result.** Transaction authorization may take several seconds, depending on factors such as connectivity for both the PSP and the merchant’s device. To ensure a seamless visual transition, display your authorization [Progress indicators](progress-indicators.md) once the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen animation concludes (for developer guidance, see [PaymentCardReader.Event.readyForTap](apple:ProximityReader/PaymentCardReader/Event/readyForTap)).
- **Clearly present the outcome of a transaction, whether it was declined or successful.** A transaction may be declined due to insufficient funds, suspected fraud, or incorrect PIN entry by the customer. Whenever feasible, also provide merchants with ways to offer customers a digital receipt, such as via QR code or text message.
- **Assist merchants in completing the checkout flow when a payment cannot finalize with Tap to Pay on iPhone.** For instance, a tap might fail if the card is unreadable, unsupported by the payment network, does not permit transactions at the specified amount, or does not allow online PIN entry. In these situations, you may:
- Display a new screen or reuse your existing checkout screen to allow merchants to accept an alternate payment method, such as cash.
- Support checkout using a different method, like external hardware or a payment link.
- Relaunch Tap to Pay on iPhone if the customer wishes to try another card.

After receiving payment card data, you might also encounter scenarios similar to those listed below. If such situations arise, consult your PSP for guidance on how to address them.

- Certain regions require Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) support, meaning that although the payment card may not need a PIN during the tap, the issuing bank can request a PIN after receiving the transaction processing request. In this scenario, your app might need to display the PIN entry screen instead of the transaction result.
- In some regions, your app may need to meet extra requirements to accommodate certain card limitations, such as those found in Offline PIN markets. Some PSPs support additional PIN fallback functionality to gather partial data from a tap, allowing merchants to continue the payment using another method like a payment link.
- **If the system returns an error that requires merchant action, display a clear explanation of the issue and suggest an appropriate fix.** For example, if the device’s iOS version does not support Tap to Pay on iPhone, present an [Alerts](alerts.md) message recommending the user update to the latest version. For developer guidance, see [PaymentCardReaderSession.ReadError](apple:ProximityReader/PaymentCardReaderSession/ReadError).
- **Make it easy for merchants to seek assistance with issues they cannot resolve.** For example, direct merchants to the help content within your app or on your website and provide an action that connects them with your support team.
