# Best practices
- **Allow users to experience your application before requiring a purchase.** Users are often more willing to commit financially to paid features or items after they have enjoyed the app and understood its value. If your offering includes auto-renewable subscriptions, you should consider providing limited free access to the content; refer to [Auto-renewable subscriptions](#Auto-renewable-subscriptions) for detailed guidance.
- **Ensure a cohesive shopping journey.** Users should not feel like they have entered an entirely different application when browsing or buying your digital goods. Present products and manage transactions using a style consistent with your app's aesthetic.
- **Employ clear and brief product titles and descriptions.** Using straightforward language and titles that do not truncate or require wrapping assists users in locating products efficiently.
- **Show the final billing cost for every in-app purchase you provide, regardless of its category.** All users must be aware of the total amount charged for any purchase they consider.
- **Only present your store when payment is possible.** If a user cannot complete payments—for instance, due to parental controls—you should either conceal your store or display a user interface explaining why the store is inaccessible. For developer instructions, consult [canMakePayments](apple:StoreKit/AppStore/canMakePayments).
- **Utilize the system's default confirmation sheet.** When a user begins an in-app purchase, the operating system presents a confirmation screen to mitigate accidental purchases. Do not alter or recreate this sheet.

#### Supporting Family Sharing
Individuals can utilize Family Sharing to extend access to purchased content—such as non-consumable in-app purchases and auto-renewable subscriptions—to up to five additional family members across all their Apple devices. To help users benefit from the Family Sharing support you provide, consider these guidelines:

- **Ensure Family Sharing is prominently mentioned in locations where users learn about your content.** For instance, including terms like “Family” or “Shareable” in the subscription or item title, and referencing Family Sharing on your sign-up screen, can highlight this feature and assist users in making an informed decision.
- **Assist users in grasping the advantages of Family Sharing and how to participate.** When you enable Family Sharing, individuals may receive notifications regarding the change, depending on their current settings. For example, an existing subscriber whose sharing setting is disabled (the default) will receive a notification from Apple inviting them to share their subscription with family members. Similarly, a family member may receive a notification about content that is being shared with them. (For details on the types of notifications users may receive, consult [Auto-renewable subscriptions](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/subscriptions/).)
- **Strive to tailor your in-app messaging so that it resonates appropriately with both purchasers and family members.** For example, when a family member accesses shared content for the first time, you might greet them using phrasing such as “Your family subscription includes…”.

#### Providing help with in-app purchases
If users need support regarding a purchase or wish to initiate a refund, you can enhance the experience by integrating custom UI within your app. This interface should offer assistance, present alternative solutions, and facilitate the start of the system-managed refund process. For technical guidance, refer to [beginRefundRequest(for:in:)](apple:StoreKit/Transaction/beginRefundRequest(for:in:)-65tph); for specific guidance related to auto-renewable subscriptions, consult [Helping people manage their subscriptions](#Helping-people-manage-their-subscriptions).

- **Provide assistance that customers can view prior to requesting a refund.** In addition to linking to the system's refund flow, your custom purchase support screen can offer app-specific help. For instance, this screen might assist users in resolving issues with missing purchases, address common questions about your in-app offerings, or provide channels for feedback submission or direct support contact.
- **Use a straightforward title for the refund action, such as “Refund” or “Request a Refund”.** Since the system-managed flow makes it explicit that the refund request is directed to Apple, there is no need to restate this information.
- **Assist users in locating the specific purchase that requires attention.** For every recent transaction displayed, include contextual details to help users identify the correct item. For example, you could show a product image along with its name and description, and include the original purchase date.
- **Consider presenting alternative resolutions.** For example, if a customer has not received the purchased item, you might offer immediate fulfillment or a compensatory item. Regardless of any alternatives provided, it must remain clear that the option to request a refund is still available.
- **Ensure the refund request process is effortless for users.** While your purchase support screen can contain helpful information and alternative solutions, this content must not impede the ability to request a refund. For example, do not require users to scroll or navigate to another screen just to reveal the refund request button. When a user selects your refund-request item, they are automatically directed into the system's refund flow shown below.
- **Do not comment on or advise regarding Apple’s refund policies.** For instance, do not speculate whether a customer will receive the requested refund. To help users understand the refund request procedure, you may provide a link to [Request a refund for apps or content that you bought from Apple](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204084).
