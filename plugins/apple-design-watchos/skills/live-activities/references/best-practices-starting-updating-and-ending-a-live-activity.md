# Starting, updating, and ending a Live Activity
- **Initiate Live Activities at appropriate times, and ensure users can easily dismiss them within your application.** Users expect Live Activities to begin and provide critical updates for a task or at specific moments, even if initiated automatically. For instance, they might anticipate an activity starting after placing a food order, requesting a rideshare, or when their preferred sports team's match commences. However, unexpected Live Activities can be surprising or unwelcome. Consider providing controls within the app view that correspond to the activity, allowing users to turn off a Live Activity. For example, a sports application might include a button enabling users to unfollow a game or team. If users cannot easily manage the appearance of Live Activities from your app, they may opt to disable Live Activities entirely in Settings.
- **Provide an App Shortcut that launches your Live Activity.** App Shortcuts expose functionality to the operating system, granting access across many contexts. For example, create an App Shortcut that enables users to start your Live Activity using the Action button on iPhone. Refer to [App Shortcuts](app-shortcuts.md) for further details.
- **Only update a Live Activity when genuinely new information is available.** If the underlying data or status remains unchanged, maintain the current display until a change occurs in the content or status.
- **Notify users only with essential updates that demand their attention.** Live Activity alerts illuminate the screen and, by default, play a notification sound to draw attention to updates that users must not miss. Alerts also display the expanded view in the Dynamic Island or a banner on devices lacking the Dynamic Island. To maximize the value provided by your Live Activities, avoid alerting users excessively or with non-critical updates, and refrain from using push notifications alongside Live Activities for the same information.
- **Allow users to track multiple events efficiently using a single Live Activity.** Instead of requiring users to switch between separate Live Activities to follow different events, prefer a unified Live Activity that utilizes a dynamic layout and cycles through different events. For example, a sports application could use one Live Activity to cycle through scored points, fouls, and substitutions across multiple matches.
- **Always conclude a Live Activity immediately when the task or event finishes, and consider setting a custom dismissal duration.** When a Live Activity concludes, the system instantly removes it from the Dynamic Island and CarPlay. On the Lock Screen, in the Mac menu bar, and within the watchOS Smart Stack, it persists for up to four hours. Depending on the nature of the Live Activity, a summary view may only be relevant shortly after it ends. Consider selecting a custom dismissal time that is proportional to the duration of your Live Activity. In most scenarios, 15 to 30 minutes is sufficient. For example, a rideshare application could end its Live Activity upon ride completion but keep it visible for 30 minutes to allow users to review the trip summary and leave a tip. For developer guidance, consult [Displaying live data with Live Activities](apple:ActivityKit/displaying-live-data-with-live-activities#End-the-Live-Activity).
