# Jump into gameplay
- **Enable gameplay immediately upon installation completion.** Players should not face delays between installing your game and experiencing it. Include as much playable content as possible in the initial installation, keeping the download time under 30 minutes. Subsequent content should be downloaded asynchronously in the background. For implementation details, consult [Loading](loading.md).
- **Supply excellent default configurations.** Users appreciate being able to begin playing without needing extensive initial setup. Utilize device information to select optimal defaults for your game, such as the display resolution that renders graphics best, automatic detection of paired accessories and controllers, and the player's accessibility preferences. Additionally, ensure your game supports the platform’s most common interaction methods. See [Settings](settings.md) for guidance.
- **Educate players through action.** Players often grasp new mechanics and information more effectively when they encounter them within the context of your game's environment. Therefore, integrating configuration and onboarding flows into a playable tutorial that quickly engages the user and provides early success can be highly effective. If you include a written guide, consider offering it as a reference resource rather than making it mandatory for gameplay. Refer to [Onboarding](onboarding.md) for more information.
- **Postpone requests until appropriate timing.** You must avoid overwhelming players with numerous requests before they begin playing. However, if your game utilizes specific Apple device sensors or accesses data (like hand-tracking) to personalize the experience, you must first secure the player's consent (see [Privacy](privacy.md) for guidance). To help players understand why the request is being made, integrate it into the scenario that requires the data. For instance, you could ask permission to track a player's hands between an initial cutscene and the first time they use hand controls. Furthermore, ensure players have invested sufficient quality time in your game before requesting a rating or review (see [Ratings and reviews](ratings-and-reviews.md)).
