# Marks
**Select a mark type based on the information you intend to convey about your data.** Bar, line, and point are among the most common mark types; for developer guidance on these and other options, refer to [Swift Charts](apple:Charts).

*Bar* marks are effective in visualizations designed to compare values across different categories or illustrate the relative proportions of different components within a whole. When visualizing data that evolves over time, bar charts are particularly useful when each value represents an aggregate, such as the cumulative steps taken in a day.

*Line* marks are also suited for depicting how values fluctuate over time. In a line chart, a single series connects all data points. The steepness of the line indicates the magnitude of change between values, aiding users in visualizing overall trends.

*Point* marks allow you to represent individual data values as visually distinct markers. A collection of point marks can illustrate the relationship between two different properties within your data, enabling inspection of specific values and aiding in the identification of clusters or outliers.

**Consider combining mark types when it enhances your chart's clarity.** For instance, if you use a line chart to track change over time, adding point marks on top of the line can emphasize specific data points. By pairing points with a line, you allow users to grasp the general trend while simultaneously focusing on individual values.
