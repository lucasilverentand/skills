# Descriptive content
- **Provide descriptions that allow users to understand the chart's function prior to viewing it.** By including titles and labels rich with information that detail a chart’s purpose and functionality, you establish the necessary context before users examine the specifics. This upfront contextualization is particularly vital for VoiceOver users and individuals with certain cognitive disabilities, as they depend on these descriptions to grasp the chart's primary message before deciding whether to explore its details.
- **Condense your chart’s main takeaway to enhance accessibility and usability for all users.** While a chart's primary role is presenting the data supporting its central message, it is crucial to summarize key findings so that users can quickly comprehend them. For instance, the Weather application uses a title and subtitle to concisely convey the predicted precipitation for the upcoming hour, delivering the most critical information without requiring users to delve into the chart's granular details.
