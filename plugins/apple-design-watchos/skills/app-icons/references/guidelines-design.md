# Design
Focus on simplicity in your icon design. Simple icons are the most easily understood and recognized by users. Fine visual details can appear cluttered when rendered with system-provided shadows and highlights, and discerning detail becomes difficult at smaller sizes. Select a concept or element that captures the essence of your app or game, making it the core idea of your icon, and express it uniquely using a minimal number of shapes. Use a simple background, such as a solid color or gradient, to emphasize your primary design; you do not need to fill the entire icon canvas with content.

- **Maintain a visually consistent icon design across all supported platforms.** A unified design helps users locate your app quickly and prevents confusion regarding multiple versions.
- **Consider basing your icon design on filled, overlapping shapes.** Foreground solid overlaps, particularly when combined with transparency and blurring effects, can lend a sense of depth to the icon.
- **Include text only if it is crucial to your brand or experience.** Text within icons presents challenges regarding accessibility and localization, often becomes illegible due to small size, and can contribute to visual clutter. If your app name is displayed nearby, including it within the icon may be redundant. Although using a mnemonic (like the app's initial) aids recognition, avoid nonessential action words ("Watch," "Play") or context-specific labels ("New," "For visionOS"). If you include text in a tvOS app icon, ensure it overlays other layers to prevent cropping by the parallax effect.
- **Prefer illustrations over photographs and avoid replicating UI components.** Photographs contain numerous details that perform poorly across different displays, at small scales, or when layered. Instead of using photography, create a graphic representation that emphasizes the content's key features. Similarly, if your app includes an interface users recognize, do not simply reproduce standard UI elements or use application screenshots.
- **Do not use replicas of Apple hardware products.** Reproducing Apple products is prohibited due to copyright restrictions.
