# Best practices
- **Identify essential, dynamic content that users want to see immediately.** While a complication allows quick app launching, its value is maximized by displaying information that feels current and relevant. A static complication lacking meaningful data is less likely to remain prominently featured on the watch face.
- **Support all complication families when feasible.** Supporting different families increases the number of watch faces where your complications appear. If you cannot display useful information for a specific complication family, provide an image representing your application—such as your app icon—to still allow users to launch the app from the watch face.
- **Consider developing multiple complications for each family.** Offering several complications allows you to leverage shareable watch faces and enables users to customize a watch face centered on an app they enjoy. For instance, a triathlon training app could provide three circular complications—one for each race segment—each linking deep into that specific part of the app. This application could also offer a shareable watch face preset with its swimming, biking, and running complications, along with custom images and colors. When users select this watch face, no configuration is needed to begin use. For guidance, refer to [Watch faces](watch-faces.md).
- **Define a unique deep link for every supported complication.** It is most effective when each complication directs the app to the most pertinent screen. If all supported complications open the same area of your application, they may seem less valuable.
- **Maintain user privacy.** Given the Always-On Retina display, information on the watch face may be visible to individuals other than the wearer. Ensure you assist users in preventing potentially sensitive data from being viewed by others. For guidance, see [Always On](always-on.md).
- **Thoughtfully determine when to refresh data.** You supply a complication’s information via a timeline, where each entry includes a value specifying the time your data should appear on the watch face. Different datasets may require different timing values. For example, a meeting application might display information about an upcoming appointment one hour before it begins, whereas a weather app might show forecast details when those conditions are anticipated. Since you can update the timeline only a limited number of times daily, and the system retains a finite number of timeline entries per app, you must select update times that maximize your data's utility. For developer guidance, see [Migrating ClockKit complications to WidgetKit](apple:WidgetKit/Converting-A-ClockKit-App#Configure-your-timeline-provider).
