# Inclusion
Inclusive applications and games prioritize users by emphasizing respectful communication and ensuring that all content and functionality are presented in ways that are accessible and understandable to every individual

## When to use
- User asks about **inclusion** on watchOS (e.g. `"how do I design inclusion for Apple Watch"`).
- User is building an Apple Watch UI that needs inclusion and wants to follow Apple's guidelines.
- User asks to audit or review inclusion in a watchOS design.
- User mentions inclusion in the context of an Apple Watch app, game, or interface.

## Quick principles
- **Consider the tone of your copy from different perspectives** — The manner in which you write conveys nearly as much meaning as the words themselves
- **Pay attention to how you refer to people** — It is generally most effective to use *you* and *your* when addressing individuals directly
- **Avoid using specialized or technical terms without defining them** — While employing specialized or technical vocabulary can make your writing more concise, it also excludes individuals unfamiliar with those terms
- **Replace colloquial expressions with plain language** — Colloquialisms are often tied to specific cultures and can prove challenging to translate
- **Consider carefully before including humor** — Humor is highly subjective and, similar to colloquial expressions, it is difficult to translate across cultures
