# Color management
A *color space* defines the colors within a *color model*, such as RGB or CMYK. Common color spaces, also known as *gamuts*, include sRGB and Display P3.

A *color profile* describes the colors in a color space using data representations, such as mathematical formulas or lookup tables, that map colors to numerical values. An image incorporates its color profile so that a device can correctly interpret the image's colors and reproduce them on a display.

- **Apply color profiles to your images.** Color profiles ensure that the colors in your application appear as intended across different displays. The sRGB color space yields accurate colors on most display hardware.
- **Use wide color to enhance the visual experience on compatible displays.** Wide color displays support a P3 color space, which allows for richer and more saturated colors than sRGB. Consequently, photos and videos utilizing wide color appear more lifelike, and visual data or status indicators using wide color can convey more meaning. When appropriate, use the Display P3 color profile at 16 bits per pixel (per channel) and export images in PNG format. Note that designing wide color images and selecting P3 colors requires the use of a wide color display.
- **Provide color space–specific image and color variations if necessary.** Generally, P3 colors and images render acceptably on sRGB displays. However, distinguishing between two very similar P3 colors can sometimes be difficult when viewed on an sRGB display. Additionally, gradients employing P3 colors may occasionally appear clipped on sRGB displays. To prevent these issues and maintain visual fidelity across both wide color and sRGB displays, you can utilize your Xcode project's asset catalog to supply different versions of images and colors for each color space.
