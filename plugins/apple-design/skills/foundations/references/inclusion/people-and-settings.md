# People and settings
Portraying human diversity is a key way your app or game can ensure universal welcome. When users see themselves reflected in the experience and its associated materials, they are less likely to feel excluded and more likely to believe it will benefit them.

When developing copy and imagery depicting people, ensure a wide range of human characteristics and activities are portrayed. For instance, a fitness application might feature exercises demonstrated by individuals across different racial backgrounds, body types, ages, and physical abilities. If portraying occupations or behaviors, avoid stereotypes—such as exclusively showing male doctors, female nurses, or heroes and villains that reinforce real-world racial or gender biases.

Additionally, evaluate the settings and objects presented. While depicting high levels of affluence may be appropriate in specific contexts, it can otherwise feel unwelcoming or make the experience seem disconnected. When contextually appropriate for your app or game, prioritize showing places, homes, activities, and items that are familiar and relatable to a broad audience.
