# A closer look at intents
When users interact with Siri to ask questions or execute actions, Siri handles the necessary language processing and semantic analysis to convert their requests into actionable intents for your application. The sole exception is a personal phrase created by the user to run a shortcut; Siri recognizes this exact phrase without requiring additional analysis or processing.

As a designer, your primary responsibility is to provide clear, actionable content that clarifies and streamlines the user experience when interacting with Siri to accomplish tasks within your app. Since some interactions occur while your app and SiriKit are communicating regarding intent handling, familiarity with relevant SiriKit terminology is beneficial. At a high level, your application processes an intent through three distinct phases: resolve, confirm, and handle.

Initially, your app and SiriKit must reach an agreement on the meaning of the request during the *resolve* phase. You can view this stage as the time dedicated to requesting all information your app requires, and if needed, seeking further details or clarification from the user. For instance, if a user requests to send a message to "Amy," and your app has multiple contacts named Amy, the messaging application may use Siri to ask for clarification on which Amy is intended. Details specific to an intent, such as the name of a message recipient, are referred to as *parameters*. In the resolve phase, you must specify which parameters are mandatory for completing an action and which are optional. For developer guidance on this process, refer to [Resolving the Parameters of an Intent](apple:SiriKit/resolving-the-parameters-of-an-intent).

The second phase, known as the *confirm* phase, occurs once you possess all the information required to execute the intent. This stage offers users an opportunity to verify they wish to proceed with the task. Tasks involving financial commitments, for example, typically require confirmation. In addition to obtaining user consent, you may also present an error during this phase if a condition prevents your app from completing the action. For example, if users use an application to order a pickup item and the location is currently closed, your app can explain why the action cannot be completed at that moment. Presenting this error during the confirm phase prevents users from reaching the payment stage only to discover their request has failed. For developer guidance, see [Confirming the Details of an Intent](apple:SiriKit/confirming-the-details-of-an-intent).

Finally, your app executes the task and informs SiriKit of the outcome during the *handle* phase. You can supply both visual and textual feedback detailing what your app did to fulfill the user's request. For example, an application that facilitates coffee orders might display a receipt detailing the order. Siri can then speak or display the information provided by your app. For developer guidance, see [Handling an Intent](apple:SiriKit/handling-an-intent).
