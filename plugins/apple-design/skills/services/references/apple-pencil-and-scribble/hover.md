# Hover
- **Use hover to help users predict the outcome when Apple Pencil interacts with the screen.** For instance, while the Apple Pencil is held above the display, a hover preview can indicate the dimensions and color of the mark the current tool will produce. To minimize distraction, avoid constantly updating this preview as users adjust the Pencil's distance from the screen. A preview that dynamically adjusts based on height is unlikely to clearly convey the resulting mark, and rapid visual changes can be highly distracting.
- **Do not use hover to initiate an action.** Unlike tapping a button or marking the screen, hovering is a relatively imprecise motion that does not require users to precisely control the distance between the Apple Pencil and the display. You must prevent unintended actions—particularly destructive ones that might require an undo operation—simply because the Apple Pencil is held near the screen.
- **Prefer displaying a preview value that falls near the middle of a range of dynamic values.** Dynamic properties, such as opacity or flow, are challenging to accurately represent at the highest or lowest limits of their spectrum. For example, previewing a brush mark created with maximum pressure could obscure the area where users are drawing; conversely, depicting a mark made with minimum pressure might be difficult for users to detect, rendering the preview an inaccurate or even invisible representation of the actual mark. **Consider using hover to support relevant interactions close to where users are marking.** For example, you could respond to a hover gesture by displaying a contextual menu of tool sizes when users perform an action like [Squeeze](#Squeeze) or press a modifier key on an attached keyboard. Presenting a menu near the marking location allows users to make selections without needing to move the Apple Pencil or their hands elsewhere on the screen.
- **Prefer showing hover previews for Apple Pencil, not for a pointing device.** While a pointing device can also respond to hover gestures, providing identical visual feedback for both devices may cause confusion. If your application design warrants it, you can limit the hover preview exclusively to Apple Pencil input. For guidance intended for developers, consult [Adopting hover support for Apple Pencil](apple:UIKit/adopting-hover-support-for-apple-pencil).
