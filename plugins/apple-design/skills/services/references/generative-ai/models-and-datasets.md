# Models and datasets
- **Thoughtfully evaluate model capabilities.** Generative models differ; some possess general knowledge, while others are trained for specific functions. It is essential to fully grasp the capabilities of any model under consideration. Obtain hands-on experience with available models and data as early as possible to guide your design process. Note that certain model types may be unavailable depending on factors such as device compatibility, network access, or battery level. For instance, the [Foundation Models](apple:FoundationModels) framework requires a compatible device with Apple Intelligence enabled.
- **Be intentional when choosing or creating a dataset.** The data selected profoundly affects the model's behavior, whether you are training from scratch or customizing an existing model. When developing and assessing your AI model, select datasets that encompass a diverse range of subject matter representations. Document the origin and collection method for your data. Ensure you possess valid licenses for any data not personally owned, and provide appropriate choices when utilizing user-contributed information. Given that most real-world datasets are imperfect, allow adequate time for testing and evaluation to proactively mitigate bias or misinformation the model might learn.
