# Printing guidelines
App Clip Codes provide the optimal experience for launching App Clips. Therefore, it is crucial to manufacture and display App Clip Codes that ensure a dependable scanning experience over time. You have the option of printing these codes yourself or engaging a professional service, such as [RR Donnelley](https://touchless.acc.rrd.com/).

Always test printed App Clip Codes before distributing them to confirm they are scannable from different angles.

- **Use high-quality, non-textured print materials.** Print App Clip Codes on matte finishes. Avoid reflective surfaces, gloss, shine, holographic overlays, or thin laminate materials. If laminating the print material containing an App Clip Code, use a matte laminate to prevent glare and reflections. For outdoor placement, utilize UV-resistant materials or coatings to guard against fading caused by sunlight, rain, and other weather elements. When collaborating with a professional printing service, employ flexographic printing for superior results. If you are using a desktop printer to print the App Clip Codes yourself, use an inkjet printer for optimal outcomes.
- **Use high-resolution images and printer settings.** When rasterizing the SVG file, set the image resolution to a minimum of 600 ppi, and print your App Clip Codes using a minimum resolution of 300 dpi. Before printing, consider leveling and calibrating your printer to guarantee high print quality, while also avoiding poor color channel alignment, inaccurate gamma values, artifacts, or printing App Clip Codes that appear elliptical or otherwise distorted. When utilizing receipt printers, print App Clip Codes as close to the paper’s maximum boundaries as possible.
- **Use correct color settings when you convert the generated SVG file to a CMYK image.** Both the [App Clip Code Generator](https://developer.apple.com/app-clips/resources/) command-line utility and [App Store Connect](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com) produce App Clip Codes as SVG files in the sRGB color space. To reproduce colors matching those of the original SVG file, convert the sRGB image to a CMYK image. When performing this conversion, use a relative calorimetric (media-relative) intent. Select the “Generic CMYK ICC profile” for CMYK printers or the “Gracol 2013 ICC profile” for CMYKOV printers, allowing for a color tolerance CIELab Delta E of 2.5.
- **If you are using a printer that only supports grayscale printing, generate grayscale App Clip Codes exclusively.** Codes generated in color and subsequently printed in grayscale may function less reliably.
- **For NFC-integrated App Clip Codes, select Type 5 NFC tags.** The embedded NFC tag must measure at least 35 mm in diameter or possess an equivalent size.
- **If you are creating large batches of App Clip Codes, thoroughly test your printing workflow and verify the printed App Clip Codes.** For instance, conduct small, low-cost print runs using a selection of codes. Print your App Clip Codes on templates that include additional padded areas, allowing you to display the encoded invocation URL and the SVG filename alongside each code for validation at the time of printing.

If you generate numerous App Clip Codes using the [App Clip Code Generator](https://developer.apple.com/app-clips/resources/) tool or [App Store Connect](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com), you will likely engage a professional printing service. In this scenario, you must manage a significant volume of SVG files. Since there is no way to determine which App Clip Code corresponds to which URL by merely looking at the code, you must employ a file that maps each SVG file to its respective invocation URL. Under all circumstances, meticulous file management, version control, and change tracking are essential to prevent faulty print runs. For further details, consult [Preparing multiple App Clip Codes for production](apple:AppClip/preparing-multiple-app-clip-codes-for-production).

#### Verifying your printer’s calibration
The quality of your printed App Clip Codes dictates whether scanning will be successful. To guarantee a reliable scanning experience when printing these codes and to prevent the use of an unsuitable printer, Apple provides [printer calibration test sheets](https://developer.apple.com/app-clips/resources/printer-calibration-test-sheets.zip) that you can use to confirm your printer's settings and print fidelity.

- **Verify the print quality of your selected color combination using the calibration test sheet that includes text boxes for each standard color pair.** Adhere to the instructions on the sheet regarding correct scaling and confirm that your printer is capable of producing high-quality App Clip Codes.
- **Verify your printer's grayscale capabilities by printing the calibration test sheet featuring two grayscale bars.** Should any of the specific gray tones appear faint or be completely absent, your printer may require calibration or may not be appropriate for producing an App Clip Code that supports dependable scanning.
