# Choosing margins and padding
Widgets must adjust their appearance to fit the screen dimensions of different devices and display areas. Provide content at suitable sizes so that your widget displays perfectly on every device, allowing the system to handle any necessary scaling or resizing. On iOS, the operating system optimizes content designed for larger devices by adjusting its size to suit smaller screens. In iPadOS, the system renders the widget at a large scale before scaling it down for display on the Home Screen.

When designing across different devices and scale factors, consult the values provided in [Specifications](#Specifications) and the [Apple Design Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/) for guidance; however, use [SwiftUI](apple:SwiftUI) in your production widget to guarantee adaptability.

- **Generally, maintain standard margins for optimal readability.** Utilize the conventional margin width—16 points for most widgets—to prevent crowding along the edges and maintain a clean appearance. If tighter margins are required, such as when grouping content for graphics, buttons, or background shapes, setting the margins to 11 points can be effective. Also, remember that widgets employ smaller margins on Mac desktops and the Lock Screen, including in StandBy. For technical implementation details, refer to [padding(_:_:)](apple:SwiftUI/View/padding(_:_:)).
- **Align the corner radius of your content with that of the widget.** To ensure your content integrates seamlessly within the widget's rounded boundaries, employ a SwiftUI container to apply the proper corner radius. For developer assistance, consult [ContainerRelativeShape](apple:SwiftUI/ContainerRelativeShape).
