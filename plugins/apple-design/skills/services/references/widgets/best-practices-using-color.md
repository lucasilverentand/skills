# Using color
- **Employ color to elevate a widget's aesthetic without distracting from its content.** While vibrant hues capture attention, they are most effective when they do not impede a user's ability to quickly grasp the widget's information. Furthermore, your asset catalog allows you to define the colors the system will utilize when rendering the widget's editing-mode interface.
- **Communicate information without depending on specific colors to convey meaning.** Widgets can display in a monochromatic scheme (with or without a custom tint), and on watchOS, the system may invert colors based on the selected watch face. Therefore, you must use text and iconography alongside color to express meaning.
- **Utilize full-color imagery thoughtfully.** When a user selects a tinted or clear appearance for their widgets, the system defaults to desaturating full-color images. You have the option to render these images in full color, even if a tinted or clear appearance is chosen. Nevertheless, using full-color images in these appearances draws significant attention to the widget, potentially making it seem incongruous with the platform. For instance, a full-color image within a widget might look out of place when the user has selected a clear appearance. Consider reserving full-color images for media content, such as an album cover in a music application's widget, and use them at dimensions smaller than the widget itself.
