# visionOS volumes
A volume allows you to present both 2D and 3D content viewable from any angle. A volume includes window management controls similar to a standard window, but unlike those in a window, the close button and window bar adjust their orientation to face the viewer as they navigate around the volume. For developer guidance, refer to [VolumetricWindowStyle](apple:SwiftUI/VolumetricWindowStyle).

- **Prioritize using a volume for rich, 3D content.** Conversely, if your goal is to present a conventional, UI-focused interface, utilizing [visionOS windows](#visionOS-windows) is usually the most effective approach.
- **Position 2D content so it renders well from multiple viewpoints.** Since a person's perspective shifts as they move around a volume, the placement of 2D content within it might appear to change in ways that could be confusing. To anchor 2D content to specific locations within the 3D environment of a volume, you can employ an attachment.
- **Generally, utilize dynamic scaling.** Dynamic scaling ensures that the volume's content remains comfortably legible and easily interactable, even when viewed from a distance. However, if the volume's content is intended to represent a real-world object (such as a product in a retail application), you may use fixed scaling (which is the default).
- **Leverage the default baseplate appearance to help users define a volume's boundaries.** In visionOS 2 and later, the system automatically makes the horizontal "floor," or *baseplate*, of a volume visible by displaying a subtle glow around its edge when viewed. If your content does not fill the entire volume, this system-provided glow helps users become aware of the volume's limits, which is especially helpful for locating the resize control. Conversely, if your content spans edge-to-edge or fills the volume's boundaries—or if you implement a custom baseplate appearance—you might not want this default glow.
- **Consider including high-value content within an ornament.** In visionOS 2 and later, a volume can feature an ornament alongside its toolbar and tab bar. You can use an ornament to minimize clutter in a volume while simultaneously highlighting critical views or controls. When you use an attachment anchor to define the ornament's location, such as `topBack` or `bottomFront`, the ornament maintains its relative position to the viewer's perspective as they move through the volume. Ensure you do not place an ornament on the same edge as a toolbar or tab bar, and restrict yourself to only one additional ornament to prevent overshadowing important volume content. For developer guidance, see [ornament(visibility:attachmentAnchor:contentAlignment:ornament:)](apple:SwiftUI/View/ornament(visibility:attachmentAnchor:contentAlignment:ornament:)).
- **Select an alignment that matches how users interact with your volume.** As a volume moves, the baseplate can either stay parallel to the floor of the user's environment or it can tilt to match the viewing angle. Generally, a volume that maintains parallelism with the floor is suitable for content that users rarely interact with, whereas a volume that tilts to match the viewer's gaze can keep content comfortably usable, even if the viewer is reclining.
