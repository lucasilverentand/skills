# Best practices
- **Provide users with multiple ways to interact with your application or game.** Beyond granting users the freedom to select their experience, it is crucial that you engineer your software to support the accessibility features individuals utilize to customize how they interact with their devices. For detailed guidance, refer to **Accessibility**.
- **Favor launching your application or game in the Shared Space or utilizing the `mixed` immersion style.** Launching within the Shared Space allows users to reference your app or game while simultaneously running other software, enabling fluid transitions between them. If your application offers a fully immersive or `progressive` style experience, launching in the `mixed` immersion mode or using a windowed view within the Shared Space grants users greater control, allowing them to decide when immersion should increase.
- **Dedicate immersion to significant moments and content.** Not every task warrants immersion, nor does every immersive task require full immersion. While users may wish to enter a different environment at times, they often prefer to remain grounded in their physical surroundings while using your app or game, and they value the ability to use other software and system features concurrently. Rather than assuming your app or game must be fully immersive most of the time, design pathways for users to immerse themselves in the specific tasks and content that make your experience unique. For instance, users might browse their albums using a familiar app window in the Shared Space, but when they wish to examine a single photograph, they can temporarily transition into a more immersive Full Space experience where they can expand the image and appreciate its details.
- **Assist users in engaging with critical moments within your app or game, regardless of the immersion level.** Cues such as dimming, tinting, **Motion**, **Scale**, and **visionOS** can direct user attention to specific content areas, whether that content is viewed in a Shared Space window or within a completely immersive Full Space. Begin with subtle cues that gently guide attention, and only strengthen these cues when there is a strong justification to do so.
- **Use subtle tint colors for passthrough.** In visionOS 2 and subsequent versions, you can apply a tint to the passthrough view to help align the user's surroundings visually with your content, while also making their hands appear integrated into your experience. Avoid bright or dramatic tints that could distract users or diminish the feeling of immersion. For developer instructions, consult [SurroundingsEffect](apple:SwiftUI/SurroundingsEffect).
