# Providing coaching
Before users can enjoy an augmented reality experience within your application, they must move their device in ways that allow ARKit to evaluate the environment and detect surfaces. Consider utilizing the integrated coaching view to instruct users on necessary actions and provide feedback during initialization. This same coaching view can also assist users in restarting an AR session—a process called *relocalization*—after the experience has been paused, perhaps because they briefly switched to another application. For details on relocalization, consult [Handling interruptions](#Handling-interruptions); for developer guidance, refer to [ARCoachingOverlayView](apple:ARKit/ARCoachingOverlayView).

- **Conceal extraneous application UI while the coaching view is active.** Since the coaching view appears automatically when initialization or relocalization begins, prepare to hide any unrelated user interface elements to allow users to focus solely on the coaching instructions.
- **If required, provide a customized coaching experience.** While you can configure the system-provided coaching view to gather specific data—such as confirming the detection of a horizontal or vertical plane—you may require additional information or wish to employ a different visual aesthetic. If you decide to design a custom coaching experience, use the system-provided coaching view as a reference point.
