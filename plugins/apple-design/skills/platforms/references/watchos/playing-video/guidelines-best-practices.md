# Best practices
- **Utilize the system video player to ensure a familiar and seamless viewing experience.** The integrated video player delivers an excellent playback experience, offering consistent behaviors and interactions that allow users to focus entirely on the immersive content. If your application absolutely requires a custom video player, reference the behavior and interface of the system video player to help provide an experience users can immediately understand. A custom implementation that deviates even slightly from the system-provided behavior may cause user frustration because users may not recognize their habitual interactions.
- **Always display video content at its original aspect ratio.** When video utilizes embedded letterbox or pillarbox padding to conform to a specific aspect ratio, the system may fail to scale the video correctly based on the current playback mode. Padding contained within the video frame can result in videos appearing smaller in both full-screen and fit-to-screen modes. Furthermore, it prevents proper video display in edge-to-edge, non-full-screen contexts, such as Picture in Picture mode on iPad.

- **Provide additional information only when it adds value.** In iOS, iPadOS, tvOS, and visionOS, you have the ability to customize a video's supplementary information by including an image, title, description, and other useful details. Generally, limit this content so that it does not obstruct media playback. For development guidance, consult [externalMetadata](apple:AVFoundation/AVPlayerItem/externalMetadata).
- **Support the interactions users anticipate, regardless of the input device controlling playback.** For instance, users expect to press Space on a connected keyboard to play or pause media across Apple Vision Pro, Mac, iPhone, iPad, and Apple TV. Similarly, users expect to navigate their media on Apple TV using the familiar, intuitive gestures of the Siri Remote. For guidance, see Keyboards and Remotes.
- **If users need to access playback options or content-specific information within your tvOS app, consider including a transport control or a dedicated custom content tab.** Since users typically access a transport control or content tab while viewing video, it is crucial to provide only the most relevant actions and information. To help users return quickly to viewing, ensure your actions require no more than one or two steps and that your content is concise. Use a transport control for playback-related actions like favoriting a video; use custom content tabs to display supplementary information or recommendations.
- **Prevent audio from different sources from mixing as viewers switch modes.** Mixed audio creates an unpleasant and frustrating user experience. Generally, audio mixes when at least one of the audio sources fails to manage secondary audio correctly. A common scenario is: While watching a full-screen video, the viewer moves it into PiP, causing the system to automatically mute the video. In the full-screen window, the viewer starts a game playing background music, then switches to the PiP window and unmutes the video. If the game does not handle secondary audio appropriately, its sound will mix with the sound from the unmuted video. For development guidance, see [silenceSecondaryAudioHintNotification](apple:AVFAudio/AVAudioSession/silenceSecondaryAudioHintNotification).
