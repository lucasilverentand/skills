# visionOS windows
The default window style is composed of a vertical plane featuring an unmodifiable background **Materials** called *glass*. It includes controls for closing, moving, and resizing the window: a close button, window bar, and resize controls. A window may also include a Share button, **Tab bars**, **Toolbars**, and one or more **Ornaments**. By default, visionOS utilizes dynamic **Scale** to maintain the visual consistency of a window's size regardless of its distance from the viewer. For developer guidance, refer to [DefaultWindowStyle](apple:SwiftUI/DefaultWindowStyle).

- **Prefer using a window to present a familiar interface and to support familiar tasks.** To help users feel comfortable with your application, display an interface they already know, reserving **Immersive experiences** for the core activities and content you provide. If your goal is to display bounded 3D material, such as a game board, consider implementing [visionOS volumes](#visionOS-volumes).
- **Retain the window’s glass background.** The default glass material allows your content to feel integrated into the user's environment while dynamically adjusting to lighting conditions and using specular reflections and shadows to convey the window’s scale and placement. Removing this glass material often reduces the legibility of UI elements and text, making them seem disconnected; furthermore, using an opaque background can make the window feel heavy or restrictive and obscure the user's surroundings.
- **Choose an initial window size that minimizes empty areas within it.** By default, a window is sized at 1280x720 pt. When initially opened, the system positions it approximately two meters from the wearer, giving it an apparent width of about three meters. Excessive empty space inside a window can make it appear unnecessarily large and may also block other content in the user's physical space.
- **Aim for an initial shape that suits a window’s content.** For instance, a default Safari window is tall because most webpages are much longer than they are wide, whereas a default Keynote window is wide due to the aspect ratio of slides. For games, a tower-building game will likely open in a taller window than a driving game.
- **Choose a minimum and maximum size for each window to help keep your content looking great.** Users value the ability to customize their viewing experience by resizing windows, but you must ensure your layout remains functional across all dimensions. If you do not define minimum and maximum sizes for a window, users might shrink it so much that UI elements overlap or enlarge it to an extent where your app or game becomes unusable. For developer guidance, see [Positioning and sizing windows](apple:visionOS/positioning-and-sizing-windows).
- **Minimize the depth of 3D content you display in a window.** The system adds highlights and shadows to views and controls within the window, giving them a sense of **Depth** and making them feel more substantial, particularly when viewed from an angle. While you can display 3D content in a window, the system clips it if the content extends too far beyond the window’s surface. To present 3D content that requires greater depth, utilize a volume.
