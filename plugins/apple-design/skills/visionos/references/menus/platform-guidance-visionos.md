# Platform guidance — visionOS
In visionOS, menus can display items using the small or large layout styles defined in iOS and iPadOS (refer to [iOS, iPadOS](#iOS-iPadOS) for guidance). A SwiftUI view allows you to present a menu in your app or game from 3D content. To ensure the menu remains visible to users, even when obscured by other elements, you can apply a [breakthrough effect](apple:swiftui/view/presentationbreakthrougheffect(_:)). Similar to macOS, an open menu within a visionOS window may extend beyond the confines of that window.

- **Prefer displaying a menu near the content it controls.** If the controlled content is too far away, users may fail to notice the menu item's effect because they must view both elements simultaneously before tapping.
- **Prefer the subtle breakthrough effect in most cases.** This effect integrates the menu presentation with its surrounding content, preserving scene depth and context while maintaining legibility and usability. When a menu overlaps 3D content and you choose [automatic](apple:SwiftUI/BreakthroughEffect/automatic) for the breakthrough effect, the system defaults to [subtle](apple:SwiftUI/BreakthroughEffect/subtle). You can use [prominent](apple:SwiftUI/BreakthroughEffect/prominent) if it is critical that the menu overlays the entire scene in your app or game, but this carries the risk of causing discomfort and disrupting the user experience. Alternatively, using [none](apple:SwiftUI/BreakthroughEffect/none) will fully occlude your menu behind other 3D content—for example, in a puzzle game where users must navigate around barriers—but this may make the menu difficult to see and access.
