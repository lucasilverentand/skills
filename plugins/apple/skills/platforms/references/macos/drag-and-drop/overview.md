# Drag and drop — overview
Depending on different factors, the drag and drop action may *move* the selected content to the destination or *copy* it. After a successful drop, moved content exists solely at the destination; copied content persists in both locations. As a general guideline, dropping selected content within the same container moves it, while dropping into a different container copies it. Dragging and dropping content between applications always results in a copy.

Users employ different interactions to perform drag and drop based on the platform. For example:

- In visionOS, users pinch and hold a virtual object while dragging it to a new location in any direction, including along the z-axis.
- iOS and iPadOS support drag and drop through touchscreen gestures, pointing device interactions, or full keyboard-access mode.
- Universal Control enables users to drag content between their Mac and iPad.
- On a Mac, drag and drop can be performed using a pointing device, full keyboard access mode, or VoiceOver.
