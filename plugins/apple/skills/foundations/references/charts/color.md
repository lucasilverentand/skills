# Color
As with other components of your interface, color in a chart can enhance information clarity, reinforce branding, and maintain visual consistency. For general advice on color usage that is accessible to all users, refer to [Inclusive color](color.md#Inclusive-color).

- **Do not depend exclusively on color to distinguish between data points or convey critical information within a chart.** While meaningful color effectively emphasizes differences and draws attention to important details, it is essential to provide alternative methods for conveying this data so that all users can interpret the chart, regardless of their color perception. One way to supplement color is by employing distinct shapes or patterns to represent different data segments. For instance, alongside using red and black or red and white hues, Health uses two different shapes in the point marks to represent the two components of blood pressure.
- **Enhance understanding by introducing visual breaks between adjacent areas of color.** For example, in a stacked bar chart arranged in a single row or column, each mark is often assigned a unique color. In this scenario, adding separators between the marks assists users in differentiating individual components.
