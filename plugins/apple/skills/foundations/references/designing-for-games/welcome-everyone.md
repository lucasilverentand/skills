# Welcome everyone
**Prioritize perceivability.** Ensure that game content can be perceived by users regardless of whether they rely on sight, hearing, or touch. For example, do not convey critical information solely through color, nor should a cutscene lack descriptive subtitles or alternative methods for accessing the content. For detailed guidance, consult:

- Text sizes
- Color and effects
- Motion
- Interactions
- Buttons
- **Help players personalize their experience.** Player preferences and abilities vary, influencing how they interact with your game. Because no single configuration suits every user, allow players to customize parameters such as font size, control mapping, motion intensity, and audio balance. You can leverage native [Apple accessibility technologies](https://developer.apple.com/accessibility/) to support these personalizations, whether utilizing system frameworks or [Unity plug-ins](https://github.com/Apple/UnityPlugins).
- **Give players the tools they need to represent themselves.** If your game allows players to create avatars or supply names and descriptions, support the full spectrum of self-identity and provide options that encompass diverse human characteristics.
- **Avoid stereotypes in your stories and characters.** Evaluate whether the depiction of game scenarios or characters reinforces real-life biases. For instance, does your game assign specific races, genders, or cultural backgrounds to enemies? Review the content to uncover and remove biases and stereotypes; if references to real-life cultures or languages are necessary, ensure they are respectful.
