# Accessibility — overview
- **Intuitive.** Your interface provides familiar and consistent interactions, making task completion straightforward.
- **Perceivable.** Your interface does not depend on a single method to convey information. Users can access and interact with your content using sight, hearing, speech, or touch.
- **Adaptable.** Your interface adjusts to how users wish to operate their device, either by supporting system accessibility features or allowing users to customize settings.

As you develop your app, audit the interface's accessibility. Utilize [Accessibility Inspector](apple:Accessibility/accessibility-inspector) to identify accessibility issues within your interface and to understand how your app presents itself to users employing system accessibility features. You can also communicate your app's level of accessibility on the App Store using Accessibility Nutrition Labels. To learn how to evaluate and indicate support for accessibility features, consult [Accessibility Nutrition Labels](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/manage-app-accessibility/overview-of-accessibility-nutrition-labels) in App Store Connect help.
