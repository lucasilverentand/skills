# Inclusive color
- **Do not use color as the sole means to distinguish objects, signal interactivity, or convey critical information.** If you employ color to communicate data, ensure that the same information is conveyed through alternative means so users with visual impairments or color blindness can comprehend it. For instance, you may use text labels or distinct glyph shapes to identify objects or their current states.
- **Do not select colors that impair the legibility of content within your application.** For example, low contrast levels can cause text and icons to merge with the background, hindering readability, and certain color combinations may be indistinguishable for individuals with color vision deficiencies. Refer to **Accessibility** for detailed guidance.
- **Take into account how your chosen colors may be interpreted across different countries and cultures.** For example, while red signifies danger in some societies, it carries positive associations in others. Verify that the colors used in your application accurately transmit the intended message.
