# iOS
- **Aim to support both portrait and landscape orientations.** Users appreciate experiences that function across different device rotations, but sometimes your application must operate in a single orientation. If this is the case, you can assume users will attempt both orientations before committing to one supported view; therefore, you do not need to instruct them on rotation. If your game is designed exclusively for landscape mode, ensure it displays equally well regardless of whether the device is rotated left or right.
- **Prefer a full-bleed interface for your game.** Provide players with an aesthetically pleasing interface that utilizes the entire screen area while correctly accounting for hardware elements such as corner radii, sensor casings, and features like Dynamic Island. If required, you may offer players the option to view your game with letterboxing or pillarboxing.
- **Avoid full-width buttons.** Buttons integrate naturally within the iOS environment when they adhere to system-defined margins and are recessed from the screen edges. Should you require a full-width button, ensure it complements the hardware's curvature and aligns correctly with adjacent safe areas.
- **Hide the status bar only when it adds value or enhances your experience.** Since the status bar conveys useful information and occupies screen real estate that many apps do not utilize, keeping it visible is generally recommended. The exception applies when you are providing an immersive experience, such as playing a game or viewing media, where hiding the status bar may be appropriate.
