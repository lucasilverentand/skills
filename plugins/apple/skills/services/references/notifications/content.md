# Content
When a notification includes a title, the system displays it prominently at the top. For notifications regarding direct conversation, the sender's name is automatically shown in the title area; for non-communication alerts, your app name appears if no custom title is provided.

- **Create a short title if it provides context for the notification content.** Keep titles brief so users can quickly grasp the message, especially on Apple Watch where screen real estate is limited. Whenever possible, utilize the title area to convey valuable information, such as a headline, event name, or email subject. If you must use a generic title for a noncommunication notification (e.g., "New Document"), it may be preferable to allow the system to display your app name instead. Use title-style [capitalization](https://support.apple.com/guide/applestyleguide/c-apsgb744e4a3/web#apdca93e113f1d64) and omit terminal punctuation.
- **Write succinct, easy-to-read notification content.** Use complete sentences, sentence case, and appropriate punctuation. Do not truncate your message; the system handles truncation automatically when necessary.
- **Provide generically descriptive text to display when notification previews aren’t available.** If users hide notification previews in Settings, the system displays only your app icon and the default title *Notification*. To ensure users understand whether they wish to view the full alert, write body text that briefly summarizes the notification content without revealing excessive detail. Examples include “Friend request,” “New comment,” “Reminder,” or “Shipment” (see [hiddenPreviewsBodyPlaceholder] for developer guidance). Use sentence-style [capitalization](https://support.apple.com/guide/applestyleguide/c-apsgb744e4a3/web#apdca93e113f1d64) for this body text.
- **Avoid including your app name or icon.** The system automatically displays a large version of your app icon at the beginning of every notification. For communication alerts, the system displays the sender’s contact image alongside a small version of your icon.
- **Consider providing a sound to supplement your notifications.** Sound can effectively differentiate your app's alerts and capture attention when users are not actively viewing the device. You may use a custom sound matching your app's aesthetic or employ a system alert tone. If you implement a custom sound, ensure it is short, unique, and professionally produced. While a notification sound enhances the user experience, do not rely on it for conveying critical information, as users may miss it. Although vibration might also be desired with alert sounds, this cannot be provided programmatically. For developer guidance, see [UNNotificationSound](apple:UserNotifications/UNNotificationSound).
