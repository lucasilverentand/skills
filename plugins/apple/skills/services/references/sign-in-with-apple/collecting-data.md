# Collecting data
Users value Sign in with Apple for its privacy and convenience. While some applications or websites may require supplementary details—such as a date of birth or geographical residence—it is crucial to minimize data requests during account setup. Maintain the trust users place in Sign in with Apple by justifying any additional data needs and clearly displaying the information received.

- **Clarify whether the additional data you request is required or just recommended.** If supplying this information is legally or contractually necessary—for instance, agreeing to terms of service, specifying country/region of residence, birth date, or complying with regional real-identity laws—ensure users understand that this data is mandatory to finalize account setup. If the additional information is not required but enhances the user experience, make it clear that the request is optional and explain how providing the data benefits them.
- **Don’t ask people to supply a password.** A primary advantage of Sign in with Apple is that users are relieved of the burden of creating and remembering extra passwords. Unless users have discontinued using Sign in with Apple within your app or website, refrain from requesting a password.
- **Avoid asking for a personal email address when people supply a private relay address.** Sign in with Apple allows users to share a private relay address that automatically forwards messages to their verified personal email. It is essential to respect this choice and refrain from overriding it by requesting a personal email address. If your customer service, retail, or other experiences require identification via email address, you must:
- Ensure users can view their private relay address within your app or website.
- Direct users to Settings > Apple Account > Password & Security > Apps using Apple Account to retrieve their private relay address.
- Utilize alternative identifiers, such as an order number or phone number collected during a purchase.
- **Give people a chance to engage with your app before asking for optional data.** As users interact with your application, you can help them discover scenarios where providing more information benefits them. For example, suggest a contact phone number for real-time text updates or social network details if you wish them to play games with friends. If users decline to provide optional information, do not restrict their account access or prevent them from using all app features.
- **Be transparent about the data you collect.** Users appreciate knowing how their shared data is utilized. One way to demonstrate transparency is by welcoming users using the name or email address they provided. This helps establish how you are using the information and, in the case of a relay address, informs them where to locate it later. If you do not display all the data provided by users, they may question why you requested it.
