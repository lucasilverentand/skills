# Privacy protection
You must seek permission to access individuals' data, and you are responsible for implementing all measures needed to safeguard that information. Once permission is granted, it is crucial to maintain user confidence by transparently detailing how their data will be utilized. For guidance on this topic, consult [Protecting user privacy](apple:HealthKit/protecting-user-privacy).

- **Provide a coherent privacy policy.** During the app submission phase, you must supply a URL pointing to a clearly defined privacy policy that users can view when they click the link provided on the App Store page. For developer assistance, refer to [App Information > App Store Connect Help](https://help.apple.com/app-store-connect/#/dev219b53a88).
- **Request access to health data only when you need it.** It is logical to ask for weight information, for instance, when users are logging their weights, but not immediately upon app launch. When your request is directly relevant to the user's current activity, you help them understand your app's purpose. Additionally, since users can modify their granted permissions, your application must prompt for access every time it requires it. For developer guidance, see [requestAuthorization(toShare:read:completion:)](apple:HealthKit/HKHealthStore/requestAuthorization(toShare:read:completion:)).
- **Clarify your app’s intent by adding descriptive messages to the standard permission screen.** Users expect to encounter the system-provided permission prompt when health data access is requested. Include a few brief sentences explaining why you require the information and how users benefit from sharing it with your application. Do not introduce custom screens that replicate the behavior or content of the standard permission screen.
- **Manage health data sharing solely through the system’s privacy settings.** Users expect to manage access to their health information globally within Settings > Privacy. Do not confuse users by creating additional in-app screens that influence the flow of health data.
