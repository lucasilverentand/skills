# Apple Health icon
The Apple Health icon signifies that an application integrates with HealthKit and the Health app. The subsequent guidelines detail how to use this icon correctly. For instructions on referencing HealthKit and the Health app in copy and UI elements, refer to [Editorial guidelines](#Editorial-guidelines). For information regarding the "Works with Apple Health" badge in marketing materials, consult [Works with Apple Health](https://developer.apple.com/health-fitness/works-with-apple-health/).

**Utilize only the icon provided by Apple.** Do not attempt to mimic or create your own version of the Apple Health icon. Download the official Apple Health app icon from [Apple Design Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/#technologies).

**Place the name *Apple Health* near the Apple Health icon.** Displaying both elements adjacent to each other serves as a reminder that the icon represents the Health app.

- **Present the Apple Health icon consistently alongside other health-related application icons.** If displayed in a view with other app icons, ensure the Apple Health icon is no smaller than those others.
- **Do not employ the Apple Health icon as a button.** The icon should solely indicate compatibility with the Health app.
- **Do not modify the appearance of the Apple Health icon.** Do not apply corner radius masks, present it in a circular format, or add embellishments such as borders, color overlays, gradients, or shadows.
- **Maintain a minimum clear space of one-tenth (1/10) of the icon's height around the Apple Health icon.** Do not composite the icon onto any other graphic element.

**Do not include the Apple Health icon within body text or use it as a substitute for the terms *Health*, *Apple Health*, or *HealthKit*.** Refer to [Editorial guidelines](#Editorial-guidelines) for proper usage of the Health app and HealthKit terminology.

**Do not display images or screenshots of the Health app.** Like all Apple imagery, these designs are protected by copyright and cannot appear in your application or marketing materials. You may include an Activity ring element within your app to display Move, Exercise, and Stand progress; see [Activity rings](#Activity-rings) for guidance.
