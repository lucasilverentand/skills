# Siri — overview
- Ask Siri to execute a system-defined task that your application supports, such as sending a message, playing music, or beginning a workout.
- Run a *shortcut*, which is a method to accelerate actions your app defines through voice or on-screen interaction.
- Utilize the Shortcuts app to modify a shortcut's behavior, including combining multiple actions into a single multi-step workflow.
- Tap a *suggestion* to execute a shortcut involving your app (Siri may *suggest* shortcuts based on the user's current context and the information you provide).
- Use Siri to manage an accessory that integrates with your app.

Siri operates across products including iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, HomePod, and AirPods, allowing users to access it almost anywhere.

When making your app's tasks available through Siri, you have multiple opportunities to tailor the user experience. Fundamentally, you customize the flow and functionality of the everyday tasks and actions you support to meet your business requirements. To reinforce this functionality throughout the user journey, you can write dialogue that matches your company's communication style and design custom UI that integrates your app’s visual identity into the Siri interface.

As you move toward integrating your application with Siri, assess the actions your app performs and study how users interact with your app without voice commands. Then, consider these steps:

- Determine the core tasks within your application that users might need to perform regularly.
- Drive user engagement by informing the system about your app’s primary functions and by supporting suggestions.
- For actions executable via voice interaction, design functional conversational flows that feel intuitive.
- Explore the different ways users might perform your app’s tasks—such as in a hands-free scenario—and the devices they might be using, like an Apple Watch or iPad.
