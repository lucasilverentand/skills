# Editorial guidelines
**Avoid using pronouns such as “she,” “him,” or “her” when referring to Siri.** It is best practice to simply use the name *Siri*. For instance, *After you add a shortcut to Siri, you can run the shortcut anytime by asking Siri*.

**Ensure proper capitalization and punctuation when using the phrase *Hey Siri*.** The term *Hey Siri* consists of two words, and it must be italicized or enclosed in quotes, with both *H* and *S* capitalized. Do not append an ellipsis to this phrase.

#### Referring to Shortcuts
**When referencing the Shortcuts functionality or application, always capitalize 'S' and ensure that *Shortcuts* is plural.** For instance, *MyApp integrates with Shortcuts to provide a quick way to get things with just a tap or by asking Siri.*

- **When referring to specific, individual shortcuts (i.e., not the feature itself or the Shortcuts app), use lowercase.** For example, *Run a shortcut by asking Siri or tapping a suggestion on the Lock Screen*.
- **Employ accurate terminology when explaining how users interact with Shortcuts within your application.** Since individuals execute shortcuts by interacting with Siri, your phrasing must closely mirror constructs such as *Run a shortcut by asking Siri* or *Run the shortcut by asking Siri with your personalized phrase* (localized as necessary). Avoid phrases like *add voice shortcuts*, *make a voice command*, or *create a voice prompt*, or any similar variation. Instead, consider phrasing like *Add a shortcut to Siri to run with your voice* (localized as necessary).

**To encourage users to create or utilize shortcuts through methods other than voice—such as automations, Home Screen shortcuts, and different other triggers—use a phrase that does not specify the particular execution method, such as *For quick access, add to Shortcuts*.**

> **Note**
> When localizing content, use translations of your app name and the term *Shortcuts*, but omit any translation for *Siri*.

#### Referring to Apple products
**Comply with Apple’s trademark guidelines.** Your application name and imagery must not contain Apple trademarks. When using text, refer to Apple products precisely as detailed in the [Apple Trademark List](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/trademark/appletmlist.html).

- Always use Apple product names in the singular form; do not render them possessive.
- Do not translate any Apple trademark, including those for Apple or Siri.
- Avoid using generic category descriptors. For instance, use iPad instead of tablet.
- Do not suggest any form of partnership, sponsorship, or endorsement by Apple.
- Where legal information is displayed in your app, attribute all Apple trademarks (including those for Apple and Siri) using the appropriate credit lines.
- Mention Apple devices and operating systems exclusively within technical specifications or compatibility details.

Refer to [Guidelines for Using Apple Trademarks](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/guidelinesfor3rdparties.html).
