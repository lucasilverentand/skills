# Continuous improvement
- **Consider ways to improve your model over time.** You should consider updating your model to align with user behavior, address feedback, incorporate new data, and utilize enhanced capabilities. Minor enhancements, such as updating a list of restricted terms, can be made frequently and independently of the application development schedule. Conversely, major enhancements or new features should be planned around regular app releases. When upgrading to a newer, more advanced base model, plan for fine-tuning, rigorous retesting, and prompt engineering. If you are training your own model, improve performance by retraining with additional data and fine-tuning. All model updates must be thoroughly tested and refined to identify and correct any unforeseen behavior.
- **Let people share feedback on outputs.** User feedback assists in identifying unexpected results and potential issues that may arise despite comprehensive testing. Furthermore, it provides users a channel to acknowledge positive aspects of your AI experience or report when outputs do not meet expectations. Establishing trust requires taking feedback seriously and addressing concerns promptly. Feedback provision must always be voluntary. To respect users' time, place the feedback mechanism in an obvious location that does not disrupt their experience. Consider offering simple methods for both positive and negative input, such as thumbs-up or thumbs-down buttons. You may also provide a mechanism for submitting detailed feedback regarding complex issues. For guidance, refer to **Explicit feedback** and **Implicit feedback**.
- **Design flexible, adaptable features.** Generative AI is a technology that advances quickly, and both models and their resource requirements are in constant flux. Think about how your app or game can accommodate improvements in capabilities and models. For instance, you might want to separate the model from the user experience so that it can be swapped out for different models over time. Lay a groundwork that permits such future adjustments while still delivering the same high-quality user experience.
