# Treatment styles
In addition to dimensions and mounting method, the system applies one of two visual treatments to visionOS widgets. Selecting the appropriate treatment helps solidify the intended user experience.

- The [paper](apple:WidgetKit/WidgetTexture/paper) style produces a grounded, print-like aesthetic that conveys solidity and integrates the widget into its environment. When ambient lighting shifts, widgets using this paper style adjust their tone (darker or lighter) in response.
- The [glass](apple:WidgetKit/WidgetTexture/glass) style offers a lighter, layered appearance that adds depth and visual distinction between foreground and background elements to enhance clarity and contrast. Foreground components remain consistently bright and readable, unaffected by changes in ambient light.
- **Select the paper style for a print-like appearance that simulates a physical object in the space.** The entire widget reacts to ambient lighting and blends naturally with its setting. For instance, the Music poster widget utilizes the paper style to display albums and playlists as if they were framed artwork on a wall.
- **Select the glass style for information-dense widgets.** Glass visually separates foreground and background elements, allowing developers to determine which parts of the interface should adapt to the environment and which must maintain visual consistency. Foreground elements appear in full color, impervious to ambient lighting changes, ensuring critical content remains sharp and legible. For example, a News widget might feature editorial images in the background with a soft, paper-like texture. Its headlines, however, remain crisp and easily readable in the foreground.
