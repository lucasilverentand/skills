# Best practices
- **Request access only to data that you actually need.** If your application requests more information than a feature requires, or if it asks for data before the user has shown interest in that feature, trust in your app may diminish. To afford users granular control over their information, ensure your permission requests are as highly specific as possible.
- **Be transparent about how your app collects and uses people’s data.** Users are less inclined to share information if they do not clearly understand your intended usage of that data. You must always honor users' decisions regarding system features such as Mail Privacy Protection and Hide My Email, and you must be fully aware of your responsibilities concerning app tracking. For further information regarding Apple privacy features, consult [Privacy](https://www.apple.com/privacy/); for guidance intended for developers, refer to [User privacy and data use](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/user-privacy-and-data-use/).
- **Process data on the device where possible.** For instance, within iOS, you can leverage the Apple Neural Engine and custom CreateML models to handle data processing locally on the device, thereby eliminating lengthy or potentially insecure round trips to a remote server.
- **Adopt system-defined privacy protections and follow security best practices.** For example, starting with iOS 15, you can utilize CloudKit to supply encryption and key management for different data types, including strings, numbers, and dates.
