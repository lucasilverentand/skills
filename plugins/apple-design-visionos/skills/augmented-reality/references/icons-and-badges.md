# Icons and badges
Apps can feature an AR icon within controls that initiate experiences based on ARKit. This icon is available for download in [Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/#ios-apps).

- **Utilize the AR glyph as intended.** This glyph is strictly meant to launch an ARKit-based experience. Never modify the glyph (beyond adjusting its size or color), repurpose it, or pair it with AR experiences that were not created using ARKit.
- **Ensure minimum clear space is maintained.** A minimum of 10% of the glyph’s height must be kept clear around it. Do not allow any other elements to encroach upon this area or obstruct the glyph in any manner.

Apps that feature collections of products or objects can employ badging to denote specific items viewable in AR via ARKit. For instance, an application selling vintage collectibles might use a badge to mark items that customers can preview at home before purchasing.

- **Use the AR badges as intended and do not modify them.** You can obtain AR badges, available in both collapsed and expanded formats, from [Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/#ios-apps). Use these images exclusively to identify products or objects that can be viewed in AR using ARKit. Never change the badges, alter their color, repurpose them, or use them with AR experiences not developed using ARKit.
- **Prefer the AR badge over the glyph-only badge.** Generally, reserve the glyph-only badge for constrained areas where fitting the full AR badge is impractical. Both badges function effectively at their default size.
- **Only use badging if your app contains a mix of objects viewable in AR and those that are not.** If all items within your app can be viewed using AR, badging is unnecessary.
- **Maintain consistent and clear badge placement.** A badge appears most effective when positioned in one corner of an object’s photograph. Always place it in the same corner and ensure it is sufficiently large to be clearly seen (but not so large that it obscures important details in the photo).
- **Maintain minimum clear space.** A minimum of 10% of the badge’s height must be maintained as clear space around it. Do not permit other elements to infringe upon this area or obscure the badge in any way.
