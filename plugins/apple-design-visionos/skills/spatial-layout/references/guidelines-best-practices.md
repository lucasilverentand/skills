# Best practices
- **Avoid displaying too many windows.** Presenting numerous windows can overwhelm users, making them feel restricted or uncomfortable by obstructing their environment. Furthermore, managing many windows complicates the process of relocating an application.
- **Prioritize standard, indirect gestures.** Users can perform an *indirect* gesture without needing to move their hand into their visual field. Conversely, a *direct* gesture requires the user to physically touch the virtual object with their finger, which can be fatiguing, especially if the object is positioned at or above their line of sight. In visionOS, users rely on indirect gestures to execute familiar standard actions. By favoring indirect input, users can interact with any object they are looking at, regardless of its distance. If you implement direct gestures, reserve them for nearby objects that warrant close inspection or brief manipulation. For detailed guidance, refer to [visionOS](gestures.md#visionOS).
- **Rely on the Digital Crown to help people recenter windows in their field of view.** When users move or turn their heads, content may drift from its intended location. If this occurs, they can press the [Digital Crown](digital-crown.md) to recenter content directly in front of them. Your application does not need to implement support for this function.
- **Include enough space around interactive components to make them easy for people to look at.** When a user focuses on an interactive element, visionOS displays a visual hover effect confirming selection. It is essential to provide ample surrounding space for an interactive component so that it remains comfortable and easy to view, while also preventing the hover effect from crowding adjacent content. For instance, if you place multiple, standard-sized [visionOS](buttons.md#visionOS), ensure their centers are separated by at least 60 points, maintaining 16 or more points of clear space between them. Additionally, controls must not overlap other interactive elements or views, as this complicates the selection of a single element.
- **Let people use your app with minimal or no physical movement.** Unless physical motion is integral to the experience, assist users in enjoying your app while remaining stationary.
- **Use the floor to help you place a large immersive experience.** If your immersive content extends upward from the floor, position it using a flat horizontal plane. Aligning this plane with the physical floor assists in blending seamlessly into the user's environment and provides a more intuitive experience.

To gain further knowledge about windows and volumes in visionOS, consult [visionOS](windows.md#visionOS); for instructions on arranging content within a window, see [visionOS](layout.md#visionOS).
