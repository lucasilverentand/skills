# Updating widget content
Widgets periodically refresh their content to remain relevant and functional, but they do not support continuous, real-time updates. Furthermore, the system may adjust update limits based on different factors.

- **Keep your widget up to date.** Determining the proper refresh interval for your widget requires understanding how frequently the underlying data changes and estimating when users need to view new information. For instance, a widget displaying tidal conditions at a beach is useful if it updates hourly, even though those conditions are constantly shifting. If users are likely to check your widget more often than you can update it, consider including text that indicates the last time the data was refreshed.
- **Use system functionality to refresh dates and times in your widget.** Since widget update frequency is constrained, allow the system to automatically manage date and time refreshes to maximize update opportunities. Select an update frequency that matches your displayed data, ensuring content appears promptly without hiding potentially stale information behind placeholders. For guidance on widget updates, consult [Keeping a widget up to date](apple:WidgetKit/Keeping-a-Widget-Up-To-Date).
- **Use animated transitions to bring attention to data updates.** By default, many SwiftUI views animate content changes. Additionally, utilize standard or custom animations with a duration of up to two seconds to signal when new information is available or when the content has changed. For developer guidance, refer to [Animating data updates in widgets and Live Activities](apple:WidgetKit/Animating-data-updates-in-widgets-and-live-activities).
