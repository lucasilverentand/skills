# Platform guidance — visionOS
This guidance assists in arranging content within your visionOS application or game windows, ensuring a familiar and intuitive user experience. For details on displaying windows in space and best practices regarding depth, scale, and field of view within your visionOS app, refer to [Spatial layout](spatial-layout.md). To learn more about visionOS window components, consult [visionOS](windows.md#visionOS).

> **Note**
> When depth is added to content inside a standard window, that content extends beyond the window's limits along the z-axis. If the content projects too far along the z-axis, the system will clip it.

- **Ensure that the most critical content and controls are centered within your application or game window.** Users often find it easier to discover and interact with content when it is located near the middle of a large window.
- **Maintain all window content within its defined boundaries.** In visionOS, the system renders window controls just outside the bounds in the XY plane. For instance, the Share menu appears above the window, and controls for resizing, moving, and closing appear below it. Allowing 2D or 3D content to intrude upon these areas can hinder the usability of system-provided controls, particularly those located beneath the window.
- **If you require additional controls that fall outside the confines of a window, utilize an ornament.** An ornament allows you to provide application controls that remain visually linked to the window without conflicting with the system's built-in controls. Examples include a window’s toolbar and tab bar, which appear as ornaments. See [Ornaments](ornaments.md) for further instructions.
- **Make interactive elements within a window easily viewable.** You must provide sufficient surrounding space around any interactive component to ensure comfortable and clear visual identification, while also preventing the system-provided hover effect from obscuring other content. For example, buttons should be placed such that their centers are separated by at least 60 points. Refer to [Eyes](eyes.md), [Spatial layout](spatial-layout.md), and [visionOS](buttons.md#visionOS) for guidance.
