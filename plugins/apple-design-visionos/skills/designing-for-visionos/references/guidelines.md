# Designing for visionOS — full guidelines
- **Space.** Apple Vision Pro provides an unbounded canvas where users can view virtual content, such as [Windows](windows.md), [visionOS volumes](windows.md#visionOS-volumes), and 3D objects, or choose to enter deeply immersive experiences that transport them elsewhere.
- **Immersion.** Within a visionOS app, users can smoothly transition between different levels of [Immersive experiences](immersive-experiences.md). By default, an app launches in *Shared Space*, allowing multiple applications to run concurrently while users can open, close, and reposition windows. Users also have the option to transition an app into a *Full Space*, making it the sole application running. While in a Full Space, users can view 3D content blended with their environment, open portals to view different locations, or enter an entirely new world.
- **Passthrough.** [Immersion and passthrough](immersive-experiences.md#Immersion-and-passthrough) delivers live video from the device’s external cameras, allowing users to interact with virtual content while simultaneously seeing their actual environment. Users control the degree of environmental visibility using the [Digital Crown](digital-crown.md) when they wish to see more or less of their surroundings.
- **Spatial Audio.** Apple Vision Pro integrates acoustic and visual sensing technologies to model the sonic properties of a user's environment, automatically ensuring audio sounds natural within their space. When an app gains permission to access information regarding the user's surroundings, it can fine-tune [visionOS](playing-audio.md#visionOS) to bring custom experiences to life.
- **Eyes and hands.** Generally, users perform most actions by using their [Eyes](eyes.md) to focus on a virtual object and executing an *indirect* [visionOS](gestures.md#visionOS) action, such as a tap, to activate it. Users can also interact with a virtual object using a *direct* gesture, like touching it with a finger.
- **Ergonomics.** Since users rely entirely on the device’s cameras for all visual input—both real and virtual—while wearing Apple Vision Pro, maintaining visual comfort is critical. The system aids in this comfort by automatically positioning content relative to the wearer’s head, regardless of the person's height or whether they are seated, standing, or lying down. Because visionOS brings content to the user rather than requiring the user to move toward the content, users can remain stationary while engaging with apps and games.
- **Accessibility.** Apple Vision Pro supports [Accessibility](accessibility.md) features including VoiceOver, Switch Control, Dwell Control, Guided Access, Head Pointer, and many others, allowing users to utilize interactions that suit their needs. In visionOS, as in all platforms, system-provided UI components include accessibility support by default, while system frameworks offer ways to enhance your app or game's accessibility.

> **Important**
> When developing your application for Apple Vision Pro, ensure you consider the device's unique characteristics and its spatial computing environment, paying particular attention to user safety. For more detailed information regarding these characteristics, consult the [Apple Vision Pro User Guide](https://support.apple.com/guide/apple-vision-pro). For instance, Apple Vision Pro must not be used while operating a vehicle or heavy machinery. Furthermore, the device is unsuitable for use in moving around hazardous areas such as balconies, streets, stairs, or other potential dangers. Please note that Apple Vision Pro is designed for individuals aged 13 and older.

### Best practices
Exceptional visionOS applications and games are both accessible and familiar while simultaneously delivering extraordinary experiences that envelop users with beautiful content, expanded functionality, and compelling adventures.

- **Harness the distinct capabilities of Apple Vision Pro.** Leverage space, Spatial Audio, and immersion to bring vitality to your experiences, integrating passthrough and spatial input from eyes and hands in a manner that feels native to the device.
- **Determine the appropriate level of immersion when designing how your app’s most unique moments are presented.** Experiences can be shown in a windowed, UI-focused mode, a fully immersive setting, or somewhere between. For every critical moment in your app, select the minimum necessary level of immersion—do not assume full immersion is required for every segment.
- **Utilize windows for experiences that are contained and UI-centric.** To assist users with routine tasks, favor standard [visionOS](windows.md#visionOS) that appear as spatial planes containing familiar controls. In visionOS, users can place windows anywhere they wish, and the system’s [Scale](spatial-layout.md#Scale) assists in maintaining legibility regardless of proximity or distance.
- **Ensure user comfort is paramount.** To help users remain physically relaxed and comfortable while interacting with your app or game, keep these fundamentals in mind:
- Display content within the user’s [Field of view](spatial-layout.md#Field-of-view), positioning it relative to their head movement. Avoid placing content in locations that require the user to turn their head or change position to interact with it.
- Do not present [visionOS](motion.md#visionOS) that is overwhelming, jarring, excessively fast, or lacks a stable frame of reference.
- Support [visionOS](gestures.md#visionOS) that allow users to interact with applications while their hands are resting in their lap or at their sides.
- If direct gestures are supported, ensure the interactive content is not too distant and that users are not required to interact with it for prolonged periods.
- Avoid prompting excessive movement while the user is in a fully [Immersive experiences](immersive-experiences.md) mode.

**Facilitate shared activities with others.** When you employ [visionOS](shareplay.md#visionOS) to support shared activities, participants can view the *spatial Personas* of others, creating the feeling that everyone is present in the same shared space.
