# Platform guidance — visionOS
visionOS includes different accessibility features, such as Head and Hand Pointer Control and Zoom, allowing users to interact with their environment in ways that suit their comfort and needs.

**Prioritize comfort.** Due to visionOS's immersive nature, interfaces, animations, and interactions carry a higher risk of inducing motion sickness or visual/ergonomic discomfort. To ensure the most comfortable user experience, consider these guidelines:

- Keep interface elements within the wearer’s field of view. Favor horizontal arrangements over vertical ones that may cause neck strain, and avoid rapidly demanding attention in disparate locations.
- Decrease the speed and intensity of animated objects, especially those visible in the periphery.
- Be cautious with camera and video movement, preventing situations where a user might feel the surrounding world is moving beyond their control.
- Avoid anchoring content to the wearer’s head, as this can induce feelings of confinement and impede the use of assistive technologies like Pointer Control.
- Minimize the requirement for large or repetitive gestures, as these can become fatiguing and may be difficult depending on the user's environment.

For further guidance, consult [Create accessible spatial experiences](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2023/10034) and [Design considerations for vision and motion](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2023/10078).
