# Best practices
- **Ensure users are aware of SharePlay support.** Since media playback experiences are often expected to be shareable, you must indicate this capability within your application's interface. For instance, the `shareplay` SF Symbol can be used to identify content or experiences that support SharePlay.
- **If your app requires a subscription, devise ways for non-subscribers to join group activities quickly.** For example, you might provide temporary or provisional access for non-subscribers, or allow an existing subscriber to issue a one-time pass to a friend. To facilitate family members sharing content in a SharePlay session, you can implement support for [Family Sharing](in-app-purchase.md#Supporting-Family-Sharing). If users can initiate a subscription during a SharePlay session, present a streamlined sign-up flow so that joining the activity does not hold up other participants. Refer to [Auto-renewable subscriptions](in-app-purchase.md#Auto-renewable-subscriptions) for guidance.
- **Support Picture in Picture (PiP) whenever possible.** On iPhone and iPad, shared videos can be viewed within a PiP window. On Mac, a shared video appears in a background window that users can bring to the foreground when they wish to watch.

**Use the term *SharePlay* accurately.** You may use *SharePlay* as a noun (e.g., "Join SharePlay") and also as a verb when describing an action within your interface. For example, in a button or sheet enabling users to share a movie viewing activity, you could use the phrase "SharePlay Movie." Do not use SharePlay as an adjective; for example, in your visionOS app, omit terms such as *virtual* or *spatial*. Furthermore, do not modify the term *SharePlay* in any way; avoid variations like *SharePlayed*, *SharePlays*, or *SharePlaying*.
