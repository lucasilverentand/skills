# Accessibility
An application or game that is inclusive is available to all users. Since individuals depend on Apple's accessibility features—including VoiceOver, Display Accommodations, closed captioning, Switch Control, and Speak Screen—to tailor their devices to specific needs, supporting these features is crucial.

It is equally vital not to assume that any impairment might prevent someone from wanting to enjoy the experience your software offers. Making such an assumption can lead to designs that restrict the potential audience for your app or game. Conversely, when you make every experience accessible, you afford everyone the opportunity to benefit from your app or game in ways that suit them.

To assist you in designing an application or game enjoyable for everyone, remember these points:

- Each disability exists on a spectrum. For instance, visual impairments range from low vision to total blindness and encompass conditions such as color blindness, blurry vision, light sensitivity, and peripheral vision loss.
- Everyone can encounter disabilities. Besides age-related impairments that most people experience, there are also *temporary disabilities*—such as short-term hearing loss caused by an infection—and *situational disabilities*—like being unable to hear on a loud train—that can affect anyone at different times.

When designing content that welcomes people of all abilities, consider the following guidance:

- **Avoid imagery and language that marginalizes individuals with disabilities.** For example, when representing a diverse group of people, include those with disabilities, and refrain from using language that frames a disability as a negative trait.
- **Adopt a person-first approach when discussing individuals with disabilities.** For example, describe an individual’s goals and achievements before mentioning any disability they may possess. If you are writing about a particular person or community, ascertain how they self-identify; for further guidance, consult [Writing about disability](https://help.apple.com/applestyleguide/#/apd49cbb2b06).
- **Prioritize simplicity and perceivability.** Favor interactions that are consistent and familiar, making tasks straightforward to execute, and ensure your content is perceptible to everyone, regardless of whether they use sight, hearing, or touch.

To gain deeper knowledge on making your app or game accessible, refer to [Accessibility](accessibility.md).
