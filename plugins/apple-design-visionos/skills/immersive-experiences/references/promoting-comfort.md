# Promoting comfort
- **Prioritize users' visual comfort.** For instance, while 3D content can be placed anywhere during a Full Space session, it is preferable to position it within the user's [Field of view](spatial-layout.md#Field-of-view). Additionally, ensure that motion is presented in a comfortable manner when the software runs in Full Space to prevent confusion, distraction, or discomfort. Refer to [Motion](motion.md) for detailed guidance.
- **Select an immersion style that accommodates potential user movements within your application or game.** Choosing the correct style is crucial because it enables the system to react appropriately to user movement. Although users may make small physical shifts during an immersive experience—such as adjusting balance, turning, or changing posture (sitting to standing)—excessive movement can cause the system to interrupt the experience. Specifically, if users might need to move beyond the 1.5-meter boundary, avoid using the `progressive` or `full` immersion styles and consider transitioning back to the `mixed` style.
- **Do not encourage movement while using a progressive or fully immersive experience.** Some users may be unable to move, or they may simply not wish to move, due to physical limitations or their environment. Design interactions that allow users to engage with content without needing to move; for example, enable the user to bring a virtual object closer rather than requiring them to approach it.
- **If you utilize the `mixed` immersion style, minimize obstruction of passthrough.** Users rely on passthrough to understand and navigate their physical environment, making it vital that virtual objects do not excessively block their view. If your app or game displays virtual content that could substantially obscure the passthrough, switch to the `full` or `progressive` immersion styles instead of `mixed`.
- **Adopt ARKit if you intend to integrate custom content with the user's environment.** For example, this is necessary if you wish to blend virtual content into a real-world setting or use the wearer's hand positions to inform your experience. If you require access to this sensitive data, you must obtain the user's permission. See [Privacy](privacy.md) for general guidance, and consult [SceneReconstructionProvider](apple:ARKit/SceneReconstructionProvider) for developer instructions.
