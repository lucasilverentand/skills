# Immersion and passthrough
In visionOS, users utilize passthrough to view their physical environment. *Passthrough* supplies live video captured by the device's external cameras, helping users maintain comfort and connection to their physical context.

Users can also employ the [Digital Crown](digital-crown.md) at any time to manage application or game content or adjust passthrough settings. For instance, pressing and holding the Digital Crown recenters content within the field of view, or double-clicking it briefly conceals all visuals to provide a clear view through passthrough.

The system also promotes user comfort by automatically altering content opacity in certain situations. For example, if a user approaches a physical object while using `mixed` immersion, the content in front of them dims momentarily, allowing clearer sight of their immediate physical surroundings. In more immersive experiences—such as those utilizing the `progressive` and `full` styles described below—the system establishes a boundary extending about 1.5 meters from the wearer’s initial head position. As their head nears this boundary, the entire experience begins to fade and passthrough visibility increases. When the head moves beyond this limit, the immersive visuals are replaced in space by the app’s icon and restore when the wearer returns to their original location or recenters using the Digital Crown.

#### Immersion styles
When your application or game enters a Full Space, the system minimizes competing apps to allow users to focus solely on yours. In a Full Space, you can present 3D content unbound by traditional windows, alongside standard windowed and volumetric content. For guidance on this process, consult [automatic](apple:SwiftUI/ImmersionStyle/automatic).

visionOS provides multiple ways to immerse users in your content, both within the Shared Space and upon transitioning into a Full Space. These methods include:

- **Use dimmed passthrough to direct attention to your content.** You can subtly tint or dim the passthrough and other visible elements, drawing focus to your app in the Shared Space without hiding competing applications or games, or establishing a more focused viewing experience within a Full Space. Although passthrough defaults to black tinting, you have the option to apply a custom color for a dynamic effect in your application. For developer guidance, see [SurroundingsEffect](apple:SwiftUI/SurroundingsEffect).
- **Create unbounded 3D experiences.** Utilize the `mixed` immersion style in a Full Space to blend your content with passthrough. When running in a Full Space, your app or game can request data regarding nearby physical objects and the room layout, enabling you to display virtual content within a person's environment. The `mixed` immersion style does not impose boundaries. Instead, if an individual approaches a physical object too closely, the system automatically renders nearby content semi-opaque to maintain awareness of their surroundings. For developer guidance, see [mixed](apple:SwiftUI/ImmersionStyle/mixed) and [ARKit](apple:ARKit).
- **Use `progressive` immersion to blend your custom environment with a person’s surroundings.** The `progressive` style in a Full Space allows you to display a custom environment that partially overlays the passthrough. You can also specify an immersion range optimized for your app or game and support portrait or landscape orientations. While in this immersive experience, users can employ the Digital Crown to adjust the level of immersion within either a default range (120- to 360-degrees) or a custom range you define. The system automatically establishes an approximate 1.5-meter boundary when the experience transitions to `progressive` mode. For developer guidance, see [progressive](apple:SwiftUI/ImmersionStyle/progressive).
- **Use `full` immersion to achieve a completely immersive experience.** The `full` style in a Full Space allows you to present a 360-degree custom environment that entirely replaces passthrough, transporting users to a new location. Similar to the `progressive` style, the system establishes an approximate 1.5-meter boundary when a fully immersive experience begins. For developer guidance, see [full](apple:SwiftUI/ImmersionStyle/full).
