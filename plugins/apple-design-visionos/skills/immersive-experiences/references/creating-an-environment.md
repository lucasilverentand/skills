# Creating an environment
When your application or game shifts into a Full Space, you have the option to replace the passthrough view with a custom environment that either partially or entirely surrounds the user, transporting them to a new setting. The following guidelines will assist you in designing an appealing environment.

- **Minimize distracting content.** To maintain immersion during a primary activity, such as viewing a video, avoid including excessive movement or high-contrast details in your environment. Conversely, if you intend to direct the user's focus to specific areas, consider employing high-quality textures and shapes in those important zones while utilizing lower quality assets and dimming less critical areas.
- **Help people distinguish interactive objects in your environment.** Users often rely on an object's proximity to determine if interaction is possible. For instance, when a 3D object is placed distant from the user, they are less likely to attempt touching or moving toward it. However, placing a 3D object near the user increases the likelihood of interaction attempts.
- **Keep animation subtle.** Gentle, minor movements, such as drifting clouds or transformations, can enhance your custom environment without causing discomfort or distraction. Always refrain from displaying excessive movement near the edges of a person's field of view. For further guidance, refer to [Motion](motion.md).
- **Create an expansive environment, regardless of the depicted location.** A small or restrictive setting can cause users to feel uncomfortable or claustrophobic.
- **Use Spatial Audio to create atmosphere.** In visionOS, you utilize Spatial Audio to reproduce sound that users perceive as originating from specific locations in space, rather than solely from speakers (see [Playing audio](playing-audio.md) for guidance). As you design a soundscape to complement your custom environment, maintain freshness and engagement by avoiding excessive repetition or looping. If users can access other audio while in your environment (e.g., watching a movie), ensure the soundscape volume is lowered or silenced entirely.
- **In general, avoid using a flat 360-degree image for your environment.** A 360-degree image often fails to convey a sense of scale when viewed in an environment, which can detract from the experience's immersiveness. It is preferable to construct object meshes that incorporate lighting and use shaders to implement subtle animations, such as the movement of leaves or clouds, or object reflections.
- **Help people feel grounded.** Always include a ground plane mesh to prevent users from feeling like they are floating. If you must use a flat 360-degree image, adding a ground plane mesh can enhance realism.
- **Minimize asset redundancy.** Overuse of the same assets or models tends to make an environment feel less authentic.
