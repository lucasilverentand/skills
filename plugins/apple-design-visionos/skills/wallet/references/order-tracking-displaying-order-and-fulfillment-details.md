# Displaying order and fulfillment details
- **Provide a link to an area where people manage their order.** When you supply a universal link, users can access your order management section even without having your application installed. For more information on universal links, refer to [Allowing apps and websites to link to your content](apple:Xcode/allowing-apps-and-websites-to-link-to-your-content); developer guidance is available at [Order](apple:WalletOrders/Order).
- **Clearly describe each item so people can verify that their order contains everything they expect.** You must use the `LineItem` property to furnish details such as a product's price, name, and image. An order aggregates the line items for every item purchased; conversely, a fulfillment only lists the items included in that specific shipment. When appropriate, attaching a PDF receipt to an individual transaction related to the order is also possible.
- **Supply a prioritized list of your apps that might be installed on the device.** The system utilizes this sequence when it needs to display a link to your application within the order details view. For instance, if multiple apps are provided and more than one is installed on the device, the system displays a link to the app highest on your list. If none of your apps are installed, the system defaults to linking to the first app on your list. See [Order](apple:WalletOrders/Order) for developer guidance.
- **Avoid sending duplicate notifications.** For example, you can instruct the system to suppress order-related Wallet notifications if the customer has one of your associated apps installed.
- **Make it easy for customers to contact the merchant.** Offer several methods of contact so users can select the most convenient option. While providing a link to the merchant’s website or landing page is the minimum requirement, you may also include a phone number, an email address, a Messages for Business link, or a support page link. When customers select the Contact button within an order, the system presents a menu of the contact methods you have supplied. Refer to [Merchant](apple:WalletOrders/Merchant) for developer guidance.
- **Help people track their order.** A multi-item order may involve multiple fulfillments, each being either shipping or pickup. For example, if a customer orders both shoes and a T-shirt, they might choose to ship one item while picking up the other. Regardless of the fulfillment type, you must provide sufficient information for users to know the location and expected arrival time of their items. In addition to an estimated delivery date, users particularly appreciate the following information:
- A link that directs the carrier’s website to a page containing details about a shipping fulfillment. When possible, provide a direct link—in addition to the tracking number—allowing users quick access to the most current shipping status. If necessary, display this link on any intermediate order-tracking page you utilize.
- A scannable barcode when pickup fulfillment requires one. Providing the barcode within Wallet is convenient, eliminating the need for users to locate it in an email or webpage.
- Clear, detailed instructions that assist customers in receiving or picking up their order.
- **Keep the fulfillment screen centered on order tracking.** For instance, if you suggest your app or other services to customers, ensure that order-tracking information takes precedence over any other content displayed on the screen.
- **Choose shipping-fulfillment values that match the details you have about the shipping process.** If you know the carrier, enter its name in the `carrier` property; otherwise, maintain the default value of “Track Shipment.” If you have access to a carrier’s interim shipping steps—such as when the fulfillment is en route or out for delivery—indicate each step using specific status values like `onTheWay`, `outForDelivery`, or `delivered`. Conversely, if you lack access to the carrier’s shipping details, use the `shipped` status. In both scenarios, provide a tracking link (if available) so users can monitor their order independently. See [ShippingFulfillment](apple:WalletOrders/ShippingFulfillment) for developer guidance.
- **Keep customers informed through relevant fulfillment status descriptions.** A high-quality status message should be accurate, accessible, and clearly reflective of the current status. Furthermore, in addition to providing information that helps users understand their order's status, a status message allows you to incorporate your brand’s unique communication style.
- **Be direct and thorough when describing an Issue or Canceled status.** Users generally require to know the reason for any problem and what actions they can take regarding it.
