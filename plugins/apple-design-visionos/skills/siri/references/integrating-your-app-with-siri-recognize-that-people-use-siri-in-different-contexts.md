# Recognize that people use Siri in different contexts
Siri can be accessed across different contexts, including while driving, exercising, using a device application, or interacting with HomePod. Since the context in which users invoke Siri to execute your app's functions is not always known, flexibility is essential to ensure a positive user experience regardless of the situation.

To effectively communicate with users across any context, provide information that Siri can convey both verbally and visually. By supporting both voice and screen-based content, Siri can determine the most appropriate communication method for the user's current circumstances. For instance, if a user says "Hey Siri" while wearing AirPods, Siri will speak to them through the device.

In voice-only scenarios, Siri must verbally describe information that would typically be displayed on screen. Consider a food delivery application requiring transaction confirmation before finalizing an order. In a voice-only interaction, Siri might state: “Your total is fifteen dollars, and your order will take thirty minutes to arrive at your door. Ready to order?” Conversely, when users can view the cost and delivery details visually, Siri may only say: “Ready to order?” When implementing custom intents, the responsibility lies with you to supply the voice dialogue that describes this on-screen information.
