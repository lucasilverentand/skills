# Best practices
- **Group related items to aid users in locating desired information.** For instance, you might employ negative space, background shapes, colors, materials, or dividing lines to indicate relationships between elements and segment information into distinct areas. When doing so, maintain clear separation between the content and its controls.
- **Ensure essential information is easily discoverable by allocating it adequate space.** Since users expect to view the most critical details immediately, do not overwhelm them by crowding important information with secondary details. You can make supplementary information available elsewhere in the window or include it within an additional view.
- **Allow content to extend across the entire screen or window.** Verify that backgrounds and full-screen artwork stretch to cover all edges of the display. Furthermore, confirm that scrollable layouts reach the bottom and sides of the device screen entirely. Controls and navigation elements, such as tab bars and sidebars, should overlay the content rather than existing on the same plane; your layout must account for this.

If your content does not occupy the full window, utilize a background extension view to simulate content existing behind the control layer on either side of the screen (e.g., beneath a sidebar or inspector). For developer guidance, consult [backgroundExtensionEffect()](apple:SwiftUI/View/backgroundExtensionEffect()) and [UIBackgroundExtensionView](apple:UIKit/UIBackgroundExtensionView).
