# Limiting notifications
App Clips allow users to schedule and receive notifications for up to eight hours following launch, providing sufficient time to follow up and finish most typical tasks.

- **Only request extended notification usage if it is genuinely necessary.** If your App Clip’s features extend beyond a single day, you must explicitly ask for permission to schedule and receive notifications. For instance, an App Clip for a car rental service could seek approval to send reminders regarding the return date of a vehicle.
- **Maintain focus with notifications.** Do not use them for purely promotional content, and only deploy notifications following a specific action taken by the user. If a user finishes their task without exiting the App Clip, notifications may be unnecessary.
- **Leverage notifications to assist users in completing a task.** Any notification related to an App Clip must pertain directly to the objective the App Clip supports. For example, a food ordering App Clip could send alerts concerning a scheduled delivery.

For developer guidance, consult [Enabling notifications in App Clips](apple:AppClip/enabling-notifications-in-app-clips).
