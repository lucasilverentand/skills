# Apple Pay
Apple Pay provides a secure and straightforward method for completing transactions—including purchases of physical items and services, as well as donations and subscriptions—across apps on iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Vision Pro, Apple Watch, websites, and any browser

## When to use
- User asks about **apple pay** on tvOS (e.g. `"how do I design apple pay for Apple TV"`).
- User is building an Apple TV UI that needs apple pay and wants to follow Apple's guidelines.
- User asks to audit or review apple pay in a tvOS design.
- User mentions apple pay in the context of an Apple TV app, game, or interface.

## Quick principles
- **Offer Apple Pay on all devices and browsers that support it** — If a device lacks support for Apple Pay, do not present it as an available payment option
- **If you utilize Apple Pay APIs to verify if a user has an active card in Wallet, you must designate Apple Pay as the primary payment method—though not exclusively so—in every location where those APIs are used** — For instance, you may pre-select Apple Pay when displaying it alongside alternative payment choices
- **If you also provide alternative payment methods, offer Apple Pay concurrently** — Ensure that Apple Pay is featured with equal prominence to other options on every page or screen where payment methods are presented…
- **If you use an Apple Pay button to initiate the payment process, you must display it using the Apple-provided API** — Unlike a simple graphic button, buttons generated by the API maintain correct appearance and automatic localization

- **Use the Apple Pay mark solely to indicate acceptance of Apple Pay** — The Apple Pay mark itself does not facilitate payment
- **Inform search engines that Apple Pay is accepted on your website** — If your site employs semantic markup to provide product details to search engines, list Apple Pay as an accepted payment option
- **Provide a cohesive checkout experience** — The entire checkout process should feel seamlessly integrated with your application or website
- **If Apple Pay is available, assume the person wants to use it** — Consider presenting the Apple Pay button as the primary payment option—perhaps by making it larger or visually isolating it from other choices
- **Accelerate single-item purchases with Apple Pay buttons on product detail pages** — In addition to the shopping cart, consider placing Apple Pay buttons on product detail pages to enable quick purchases of individual items
