# Streamlining checkout — overview
- **Provide a cohesive checkout experience.** The entire checkout process should feel seamlessly integrated with your application or website. To reinforce this integration, utilize your branding throughout the flow and prevent users from navigating between different pages or windows. For web checkouts specifically, opening new windows during the process can cause user confusion and may lead customers to believe they have been transferred to a different site.
- **If Apple Pay is available, assume the person wants to use it.** Consider presenting the Apple Pay button as the primary payment option—perhaps by making it larger or visually isolating it from other choices.
- **Accelerate single-item purchases with Apple Pay buttons on product detail pages.** In addition to the shopping cart, consider placing Apple Pay buttons on product detail pages to enable quick purchases of individual items. Purchases initiated this way must pertain only to a single item, excluding any goods already in the cart. If an item purchased via the product detail page is present in the shopping cart, remove it upon successful completion of the purchase.
- **Accelerate multi-item purchases with express checkout.** Consider implementing an express checkout feature that immediately displays the payment sheet, enabling users to quickly purchase multiple items using a single shipping address and method. If you offer coupons or promotional codes, entering them on the payment sheet can enhance this express checkout experience.
- **Collect necessary information, like color and size options, before people reach the Apple Pay button.** If additional details are required during checkout—such as a missing option selection—gracefully address the issue and assist the user in making the correction. Use warning text or highlighting to indicate missing data, and automatically navigate to the relevant field so the user can quickly correct it and finalize the purchase.
- **Collect optional information before checkout begins.** Since the payment sheet does not allow input for optional data (e.g., delivery instructions or gift messages), gather this information either before checkout starts or after the purchase is complete.
- **Gather multiple shipping methods and destinations before showing the payment sheet.** The payment sheet facilitates selection of a single shipping method and destination for the entire order. If your customers can select different shipping methods or destinations for individual items within an order, collect these details before the Apple Pay checkout begins.
- **For in-store pickup, help people choose a pickup location before displaying the payment sheet.** Once the customer has selected their desired pickup location, display the address on the payment sheet using a read-only format. For developer guidance, consult [Displaying a Read-Only Pickup Address](apple:PassKit/displaying-a-read-only-pickup-address).
- **Prefer information from Apple Pay.** Assume that the data provided by Apple Pay is complete and current. Even if your app or website holds existing contact, shipping, and payment details, consider fetching the most recent information from Apple Pay during checkout to minimize potential corrections.
- **Avoid requiring account creation prior to purchase.** If you wish users to register, prompt them to do so on the order confirmation page. Prepopulate as many registration fields as possible using information provided by the payment sheet during checkout.
- **Report the result of the transaction so that people can view it in the payment sheet.** In cases of failure, the payment sheet should display the errors you provide, allowing users to take steps toward resolution.
- **Display an order confirmation or thank-you page.** After the payment sheet displays the transaction result, present an order confirmation or thank-you page. This page should thank the customer for their purchase, provide shipping details, and explain how to track the order status. Listing Apple Pay on this confirmation page is optional; if included, display it following the last four digits of the account used or as a separate note. For instance: ”1234 (Apple Pay)” or ”Paid with Apple Pay.”
