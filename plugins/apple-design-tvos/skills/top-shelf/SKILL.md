# Top Shelf
The Apple TV Home Screen features Top Shelf, an area designed to showcase your content engagingly while also allowing users access to their preferred apps via the Dock

## When to use
- User asks about **top shelf** on tvOS (e.g. `"how do I design top shelf for Apple TV"`).
- User is building an Apple TV UI that needs top shelf and wants to follow Apple's guidelines.
- User asks to audit or review top shelf in a tvOS design.
- User mentions top shelf in the context of an Apple TV app, game, or interface.

## Quick principles
- **Help users quickly access your content** — Top Shelf offers a direct pathway to the most relevant material
- **Feature new material** — For instance, promote recent releases or episodes, feature upcoming movies and shows, and refrain from promoting content users have already purchased, rented…
- **Tailor the experience to individual preferences** — Since users often place their most-used apps in Top Shelf, you can customize their experience by providing targeted recommendations within the Top…
- **Minimize advertisements and pricing displays** — Users place your app in Top Shelf because they are already interested, so excessive ads from your application may be unwelcome
- **Present engaging dynamic content that encourages deeper viewing** — Although static images are permissible, users generally prefer a captivating, dynamic Top Shelf experience that features the newest or highest-rated content
- **If you do not supply the recommended full-screen content, provide at least one static image as a fallback** — The system displays a static image when your app is in the Dock and focused, provided full-screen content is unavailable
- **Do not suggest interactivity using a static image** — A static Top Shelf image is not focusable, and you must prevent users from believing it is interactive
- **Provide a title** — Use a concise main title, such as the name of a film, television series, or photo collection
- **Provide a title that identifies the currently playing content** — The content's title is displayed near the top of the screen to ensure it is immediately readable
- **Ensure sufficient content to constitute a complete row** — As a minimum requirement, load enough images in the sectioned content row to span the entire screen width
- **Be mindful of scaling adjustments when combining different image dimensions** — If your Top Shelf design incorporates a mix of image sizes, be aware that images will automatically scale to match the height…
- **Provide three to eight images** — For the scrolling banner to be most effective, a minimum of three images is advised
