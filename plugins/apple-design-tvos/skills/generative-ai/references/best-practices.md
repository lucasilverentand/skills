# Best practices
- **Design your experience responsibly.** Responsible AI involves the deliberate design and development of AI features, taking into account their direct and indirect effects on individuals, systems, and society. While generative AI allows for rapid prototyping of exciting new app features, creating a robust experience that functions reliably in all real-world scenarios remains challenging. Unlike traditional programming, minor input variations (or even repeated identical inputs) can yield vastly different results with generative AI. Furthermore, anticipating all user requests and the corresponding AI responses is often impossible. Therefore, orient your design process toward crafting AI experiences that are inclusive, thoughtfully designed, and protect user privacy.
- **Keep people in control.** Although AI can generate and manipulate content, you must respect users' agency and ensure they maintain command over decision-making and the overall experience. Honor their requests when they are within scope and the expected output is unambiguous, while handling sensitive content with caution. Provide users with the ability to dismiss unwanted new content and to revert or retry transformations or actions they disagree with. Always clearly disclose when and where AI is being utilized.
- **Ensure an inclusive experience for all.** AI models learn from data and naturally tend to favor the most prevalent information. This can unintentionally introduce harmful biases or stereotypes. Exercise extra diligence when designing your AI feature to consider how assumptions and personal attributes might affect the intended functionality. For instance, if generating images or descriptions of individuals, prompt users to supply the necessary information for the feature to function well rather than relying solely on inferring personal or cultural traits. Seek absolute clarity before making assumptions that could lead to common stereotypes, such as regarding gender identity or relationship status. Test your feature across a diverse user base to identify and correct stereotypes, ensuring inclusive outcomes. For guidance, consult [Inclusion](inclusion.md) and [Accessibility](accessibility.md).
- **Design engaging and useful generative features.** Generative AI is a potent tool, but it is not suitable for every scenario. Offer generative features only when and where they provide distinct and specific value, such as saving time, improving communication, or enhancing creativity.
- **Ensure a great experience even when generative features aren’t available or people opt not to use them.** In some instances, generative AI may be integral to the experience, leaving no practical non-AI substitute. In other cases, AI might serve a complementary role that enhances your app’s core functionality but is not critical for users achieving their goals. For example, Genmoji provides an enjoyable way to create novel emojis, but users can still utilize standard emoji. The Apple Intelligence summarization feature accelerates catching up on notifications, yet users retain the option to read notifications without it. Whenever possible, consider offering a non-AI fallback so users can always enjoy your app or game.
