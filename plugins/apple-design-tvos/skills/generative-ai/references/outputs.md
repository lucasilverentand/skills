# Outputs
- **Assist users in refining prompts when encountering blocked or suboptimal results.** Minimize undesirable or out-of-scope output by coaching users on how to achieve better outcomes next time. For instance, if a prompt triggers harmful content generation, Image Playground may respond with "Unable to use that description." Whenever possible, consider suggesting example prompts that could yield superior results.
- **Mitigate unintended and harmful outcomes through careful design and rigorous testing.** While users generally operate with positive intent, harmful results can still occur due to accidental or intentional misuse, particularly when addressing sensitive subjects. Although every potential harmful scenario may be impossible to prevent, proactively identifying risks, establishing policies to address them, and evaluating features can minimize the likelihood of misuse and harm. Consider all ways users might interact with your feature and test those scenarios. Challenge both your policies and expected use cases. Test inputs that are out-of-scope, unrelated to the app's intended experience, or poorly represented in the model’s training data. Try prompts that are ambiguously phrased, vague, or include personal, sensitive, or controversial topics; also, encourage harmful or incorrect outputs. Use these findings to enhance your model, inform prevention strategies, and respond thoughtfully.
- **Aim to prevent the reproduction of copyrighted material.** Large AI models are trained on extensive datasets sourced from the internet and other locations. Consequently, most generative models are familiar with and may unintentionally reproduce content similar to published works, including copyrighted material. You can decrease the probability of copyright infringement by building upon existing models that offer protection and by meticulously curating inputs. For example, you could allow users to select from a predefined set of prompts. Alternatively, you may explicitly instruct the model to refrain from mimicking specific content or styles.
- **Account for processing time in your design.** *Latency* refers to the duration required for a model to generate an output. Non-generative models, such as [people tracking in ARKit](apple:arkit/capturing-body-motion-in-3d) and the [Vision](apple:Vision) machine learning framework, typically exhibit low latency and are suitable for real-time execution on camera feeds. Generative models usually require more time to produce a result, so design a loading experience or generate content in the background while the user interacts with another part of the application. For detailed guidance, consult [Loading](loading.md).
- **Evaluate offering users alternative versions of results.** Depending on your feature's design, presenting a single outcome or several meaningfully distinct outcomes may be most effective. Providing users with a choice grants them greater control and helps bridge the gap between the model's interpretation and the user's actual intent. For example, Image Playground can generate multiple images representing a subject, allowing the user to select their preference. For guidance, see [Multiple options](machine-learning.md#Multiple-options).
