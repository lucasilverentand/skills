# Playing audio — full guidelines — overview
- **Silence.** Users enable silent mode to prevent unwanted interruptions from unexpected sounds, such as incoming message tones or ringtones. In this state, they also expect nonessential audio—like sound effects, keyboard clicks, or game soundtracks—to be muted. When silent mode is active, the device only reproduces audio that users explicitly start, such as media playback, alarms, or audio/video messages.
- **Volume.** Users expect that their volume adjustments will influence all system sounds—including music and in-app sound effects—regardless of the method used to change the volume. An exception exists for the iPhone's ringer volume, which can be managed independently in Settings.
- **Headphones.** Users employ headphones for private listening and, occasionally, hands-free operation. When connecting headphones, users expect sound to seamlessly reroute without interruption; conversely, upon disconnecting, they anticipate playback will pause instantly.
