# Thresholds and sizes
Widgets on Apple Vision Pro are designed to adapt based on a user's proximity. visionOS provides two critical viewing thresholds for your design: the [simplified](apple:WidgetKit/LevelOfDetail/simplified) threshold, used when viewed from afar, and the [default](apple:WidgetKit/LevelOfDetail/default) threshold, used when viewed up close.

Since widgets exist within a user's environment, it is crucial to match the widget's size to its content type and consider how it will appear at different distances.

- **Design a responsive layout that shows the right level of detail for each of the two thresholds.** When viewed remotely, display a simplified version with fewer details and larger type; interactive elements such as buttons or toggles must be removed. When viewed nearby, present more detail and use a smaller type size. To ensure a seamless and cohesive experience, maintain shared elements across both distance states.
- **Offer widget family sizes that fit a person’s surroundings well.** Widgets correspond to physical dimensions and maintain a permanent presence in the user's spatial setting. Consider where users might place your widget—whether mounted on a wall, resting on a sideboard, or near a workspace—and select a family size appropriate for that context. For instance, you might offer a small system widget suitable for a desk or an extra-large version allowing users to decorate their space with visually rich content like artwork or photography.
- **Display content in a way that remains legible from a range of distances.** To ensure the widget feels intentional and proportional to its placement, users can scale it between 75 and 125 percent. Apply print design principles—including strong typography, clear hierarchy, and scale—to ensure your content is glanceable. Include high-resolution assets that render well regardless of the scaling size.
