# Enabling Tap to Pay on iPhone
Before your application enables Tap to Pay on iPhone or configures a merchant's device, the merchant must agree to the applicable terms and conditions. Utilize the ProximityReader API to check the current status and only present the acceptance flow when necessary. For developer guidance, refer to [Adding support for Tap to Pay on iPhone to your app](apple:ProximityReader/adding-support-for-tap-to-pay-on-iphone-to-your-app#Display-the-Terms-and-Conditions-interface-to-the-merchant).

- **Ensure merchants accept the Tap to Pay on iPhone terms and conditions before they begin interacting with their customers.** Merchants must agree to the terms and conditions prior to any initial device configuration. This ensures a smooth experience if they complete this step before starting checkout or other customer-facing processes. For instance, you can include buttons within your [in-app messaging](https://developer.apple.com/tap-to-pay/marketing-guidelines/#in-your-app) or onboarding flows allowing users to accept the Tap to Pay on iPhone terms and conditions.
- **Present the Tap to Pay on iPhone terms and conditions exclusively to an administrative user.** If a non-administrator attempts to activate the feature, display a message indicating that administrator privileges are required. If your application's primary users are enterprise or non-administrative, allow an administrator to accept the Tap to Pay on iPhone terms and conditions via a web interface or another application, including those running on devices other than an iPhone. Contact your PSP for specific implementation details.
- **If necessary, assist merchants in ensuring their device is current.** If your PSP mandates specific iOS versions, ensure that you present the terms and conditions only after the merchant has updated their device.
