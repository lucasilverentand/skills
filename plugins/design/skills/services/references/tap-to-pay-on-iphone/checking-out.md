# Checking out
- **Provide Tap to Pay on iPhone as a checkout option whether the feature is enabled or not.** Including a Tap to Pay on iPhone button grants merchants flexibility by allowing them to utilize the feature without interrupting the checkout process. When a merchant selects this button, present any necessary terms and conditions and automatically display the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen once configuration is complete.
- **Avoid making merchants wait to use Tap to Pay on iPhone.** Beyond completing the initial setup for each device, a subsequent configuration is required every time your application returns to the foreground. To minimize potential delays, prepare this feature as soon as your app launches and immediately following every transition back to the foreground. Refer to [prepare(using:)](apple:ProximityReader/PaymentCardReader/prepare(using:)) for developer guidance.
- **Make sure the Tap to Pay on iPhone checkout option is available even if configuration is continuing in the background.** Merchants must always have the ability to select the Tap to Pay on iPhone checkout option within a checkout flow. While configuration is ongoing, allow merchants to select the checkout option and then display a progress indicator—do not wait for configuration completion before making this option available. In most situations, an indeterminate progress indicator suffices; however, if the ProximityReader API indicates that configuration is still in progress, display a determinate progress indicator. See **Progress indicators** for guidance and [PaymentCardReader.Event.updateProgress(_:)](apple:ProximityReader/PaymentCardReader/Event/updateProgress(_:)) for developer details.
- **If your app supports multiple payment-acceptance methods, make the Tap to Pay on iPhone button easy to find.** Do not require merchants to scroll to locate this feature. If your app does not support other payment acceptance options, automatically launch Tap to Pay on iPhone when checkout begins.
- **Make it easy for merchants to switch between Tap to Pay on iPhone and the hardware accessories you support.** Although your implementation of Tap to Pay on iPhone is distinct from your support for a hardware accessory (such as a Bluetooth chip and PIN card reader), you can enhance the user experience by allowing merchants to configure both methods simultaneously. After setup, ensure merchants can select the appropriate payment acceptance method during checkout without needing to navigate to your app settings.

**For the label of the button that activates the feature, use “Tap to Pay on iPhone” or, if space is constrained, “Tap to Pay.”** The exception applies if Tap to Pay on iPhone is the sole payment acceptance method you support; in this case, you may reuse your existing Charge or Checkout buttons to activate the feature. If you support multiple payment acceptance methods and use icons on the activating buttons, utilize `wave.3.right.circle` or `wave.3.right.circle.fill` **SF Symbols** for the Tap to Pay on iPhone button. Always refrain from including the Apple logo in Tap to Pay on iPhone buttons.
> **Important**
> Reserve the “Tap to Pay on iPhone” label exclusively for payment actions. For language used in nonpayment contexts, consult [Additional interactions](#Additional-interactions).

- **Design your Tap to Pay on iPhone button to match the other buttons in your app.** Although you must adhere to the labels “Tap to Pay on iPhone” or “Tap to Pay” as specified above, you have the freedom to use button color and shape that best coordinates with your interface.
- **Determine the final amount that customers need to pay before merchants initiate the Tap to Pay on iPhone experience.** For instance, if your app supports tipping or other customer interactions that influence the total, ensure merchants offer these interactions before displaying the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen. The goal is to display the final amount due in the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen.
- **If you support pre-payment options in your checkout flow, display them before the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen.** For example, if you allow customers to select different payment types, these options should be presented on your checkout screen after a merchant taps the Tap to Pay on iPhone button but before the Tap to Pay on iPhone screen opens.
