# Using HomeKit icons
Use the HomeKit icon when creating setup guides or instructional materials related to HomeKit technology.

Additionally, you may utilize the Apple Home app icon when referencing the application itself or within a button that launches it, including the App Store listing [product page](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/home/id1110145103?mt=8).

**Only use icons provided by Apple.** Do not create your own designs or attempt to imitate the official HomeKit or Home app icons. Download HomeKit icons from [Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/).

#### Styles
There are multiple ways to render the HomeKit icon.

##### Black HomeKit icon
When other technology icons are displayed in black, render the HomeKit icon against a white or light background.

##### White HomeKit icon
Render the HomeKit icon against black or dark backgrounds when other technology icons are displayed in white.

##### Custom color HomeKit icon
When other technology icons share a color, utilize a custom color for the HomeKit icon.

- **Position the HomeKit icon consistently with other technology icons.** If other technology icons are enclosed in shapes, apply the same treatment to the HomeKit icon.
- **Use the HomeKit icon noninteractively.** Do not include the icon and the term *HomeKit* within custom interactive elements or buttons. Instead, use the Apple Home app icon to link to the app’s product page in the App Store. **Do not embed the HomeKit icon within text or use it as a substitute for the word HomeKit.** Refer to [Referring to HomeKit](#Referring-to-HomeKit) for instructions on how to properly reference HomeKit in written content. **Pair the icon with the name *HomeKit* accurately.** If other technologies are referenced this way, you may display the name either beneath or adjacent to the icon. Ensure it uses the same typeface as your overall layout. For further guidance, consult [Referring to HomeKit](#Referring-to-HomeKit).
