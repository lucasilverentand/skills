# Creating Live Activity layouts
- **Accommodate different screen sizes and display modes.** Live Activities must scale to fit diverse device screens. Design layouts and assets for multiple devices and scaling factors, acknowledging that the rendered size on screen may fluctuate. To ensure optimal appearance across all contexts, reference the values provided in [Specifications](#Specifications) and supply content sized appropriately.
- **Optimize element dimensions and positioning for efficient spatial utilization.** Design a layout that occupies only the necessary space required to clearly present its content. Adjust the size and placement of elements within your Live Activity so they integrate cohesively.
- **Employ established layouts for custom views and designs.** Templates, including default system margins and recommended text sizes, are available in [Apple Design Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/). Utilizing these resources helps your Live Activity maintain legibility at a glance and align with the visual conventions of its environment; for instance, consider the Smart Stack on Apple Watch.
- **Maintain uniform margins and concentric placement.** Ensure that even, matching margins exist between rounded shapes and the Live Activity edges, including corners, to guarantee a harmonious fit. This prevents elements from encroaching upon the rounded boundary of the Live Activity and causing visual dissonance. For example, when positioning a rounded rectangle near a corner of your Live Activity, match its corner radius to the outer corner radius of the Live Activity by subtracting the margin and applying the correct radius using a SwiftUI container. For implementation guidance, consult [ContainerRelativeShape](apple:SwiftUI/ContainerRelativeShape).

Keep content compact and contained within a margin that is concentric with the Live Activity's outer boundary.
**When segmenting a body of content, enclose it within an inset container shape or utilize a defined dividing line.** Do not allow content to extend fully to the edge of the Dynamic Island.
> **Tip**
> If you are aligning non-rounded content within the curved corners of the Live Activity view, it may be beneficial to blur that non-rounded content in your drawing application. Once blurred, finding the positioning that best aligns with the view's outer perimeter may become easier.
**Dynamically modify the Live Activity height on the Lock Screen or in expanded view.** When less information is being displayed, reduce the Live Activity's height to utilize only the space required for the current content. As more information becomes available, increase the height to accommodate additional details. For example, a rideshare application might display a more compact Live Activity while awaiting driver assignment, but its height expands as details like the estimated pickup time and driver information become available.
