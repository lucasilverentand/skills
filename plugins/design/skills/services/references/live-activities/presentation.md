# Presentation
Your Live Activity must function correctly across all devices, locations, and display contexts. Since its appearance varies depending on the system it runs on, develop Live Activity layouts optimized for each specific environment.

**Begin development using the iPhone display as the foundation, then adapt it for other contexts.** First, establish baseline designs for each presentation mode. Subsequently, based on the specific functionality of your Live Activity, create supplementary custom layouts for contexts such as iPhone in StandBy, CarPlay, or Apple Watch. For detailed information regarding custom layouts, consult [StandBy](#StandBy), [CarPlay](#CarPlay), and [watchOS](#watchOS).

#### Compact presentation
- **Prioritize critical information.** The compact presentation must convey essential, dynamic, and easily digestible updates relevant to the Live Activity. For instance, a sports application might display both team logos alongside the current score.
- **Maintain visual and informational unity within the Dynamic Island compact view.** Although the TrueDepth camera physically divides the leading and trailing elements, design them to function as a cohesive unit. Use consistent color palettes and typography to establish a visual connection between both sections.
- **Optimize content density and proximity to the TrueDepth camera.** Keep the content as narrow as possible, flush against the TrueDepth camera. Avoid obstructing vital information within the status bar and refrain from adding padding between the content and the TrueDepth camera. Ensure a balanced layout by using similarly sized views for both leading and trailing elements; this may require employing abbreviated units or less granular data to maintain proper width and symmetry. **Provide deep links to the application.** Tapping a compact Live Activity must launch your app directly into the corresponding details. Verify that both leading and trailing elements link to the identical screen within the application.

#### Minimal presentation
**Ensure your Live Activity is identifiable in its minimal presentation.** Whenever possible, display dynamic information rather than just the app's logo, while still allowing users to quickly recognize your application. For example, the Timer app’s minimal Live Activity shows the remaining time instead of a static icon.

#### Expanded presentation
- **Maintain the relative placement of elements to create a coherent layout between presentations.** The expanded view should be understood as an enlarged rendition of the compact or minimal presentation. When the Live Activity scales up, ensure that both its information and layout expand in a predictable manner.
- **Wrap content tightly around the TrueDepth camera.** Position the content near the TrueDepth camera, and strive to minimize surrounding empty space. This approach maximizes spatial efficiency while also helping to reduce the visual prominence of the camera itself.

#### Lock Screen presentation
- **Do not replicate notification layouts.** Instead, create a unique design tailored specifically to the information presented in the Live Activity.
- **Select colors suitable for a personalized Lock Screen.** Since users customize their Lock Screens with wallpapers, tints, and widgets, use custom background or tint colors and opacity judiciously to ensure the Live Activity complements the aesthetic while remaining legible.
- **Ensure your design, assets, and color choices maintain visual quality and sufficient contrast in both Dark Mode and on an Always-On display.** By default, a Live Activity displayed on the Lock Screen utilizes a light background in light appearance and a dark background in dark appearance. If you implement a custom background color, select one that performs adequately across both modes or provide distinct colors for each appearance. It is crucial to validate your choices on a device configured with an Always-On display and reduced luminance, as the system dynamically adjusts colors in this state. For further guidance, consult **Dark Mode** and **Always On**; developer resources are available at [About asset catalogs](https://help.apple.com/xcode/mac/current/#/dev10510b1f7).
- **Validate the generated color of the dismiss button.** The system automatically determines a matching dismiss button based on your Live Activity's background and foreground colors. Confirm that this generated color aligns with your design, making adjustments if necessary using [activitySystemActionForegroundColor(_:)](apple:SwiftUI/View/activitySystemActionForegroundColor(_:)).
- **Apply standard margins to align your design with notifications.** The established layout margin for Live Activities on the Lock Screen is 14 points. Although tighter margins may suit specific elements like graphics or interactive buttons, avoid crowding the boundaries to prevent a cluttered appearance. For developer details, refer to [padding(_:_:)](apple:SwiftUI/View/padding(_:_:)).

#### StandBy presentation
- **Update your layout for StandBy.** Ensure assets render well when scaled up, and consider developing a unique arrangement that leverages the available extra screen real estate. For implementation guidance, consult [Creating custom views for Live Activities](apple:ActivityKit/creating-custom-views-for-live-activities).
- **Consider using the default background color in StandBy.** This default color allows your Live Activity to blend smoothly with the device bezel, providing a softer aesthetic that matches the user's environment. Furthermore, it enables the system to scale the Live Activity slightly larger since it does not need to account for the margins surrounding the TrueDepth camera.
- **Use standard margins and avoid extending graphic elements to the edge of the screen.** If you omit proper margins, content will be cropped as the Live Activity expands, resulting in a disjointed appearance.
- **Verify your design in Night Mode.** When operating in Night Mode, the system applies a red tint to your Live Activity. Confirm that your design utilizes colors that maintain adequate contrast under these conditions.
