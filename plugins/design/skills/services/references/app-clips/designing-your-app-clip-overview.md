# Designing your App Clip — overview
- **Enable users to complete a task or demo within your App Clip.** Do not mandate the installation of the full application for users to experience a demonstration, complete a task, or finish a game level.
- **Concentrate on core functionalities.** Interactions within App Clips must be swift and targeted. Restrict features to only those required to achieve the immediate goal. Reserve advanced or complex functionalities for the main application. If you offer a trial version of your full app, ensure the App Clip focuses on essential features that accurately convey the game or application's capabilities.
- **Do not use App Clips exclusively for marketing.** App Clips must deliver tangible value and assist users in completing tasks. Do not use them merely to advertise services or products, nor should they display advertisements within the App Clip.
- **Avoid using web views in your App Clip.** App Clips rely on native frameworks and components to deliver a high-quality app experience. If your functionality is limited to web components, provide a direct link to your website instead of an App Clip.
- **Design a linear, focused, and intuitive user interface.** App Clips do not require tab bars, complex navigation structures, or settings menus. Minimize the number of screens and entry forms. Eliminate non-essential information and reduce UI complexity wherever possible.
- **Display the most relevant content upon launch.** Bypass unnecessary steps and immediately direct users to the part of the App Clip that matches their current context.
- **Ensure immediate usability of your App Clip.** The App Clip must contain all necessary assets, omit splash screens, and launch without delay.
- **Keep your App Clip size minimal.** The smaller the App Clip, the faster it launches on a user's device. Maintaining a small footprint is critical when bandwidth is constrained. Reduce unused assets and code as much as possible. Avoid downloading additional data, which can detract from the feeling of immediacy.
- **Ensure your App Clip is shareable.** When a link to an App Clip is shared in the Messages app, recipients can launch it directly from within Messages. Provide the capability to share links to specific locations within your App Clip and encourage users to share it with others.
- **Facilitate easy payment for services or products.** Entering payment details can be a lengthy and error-prone process. Consider supporting **Apple Pay** to enable express checkout and allow users to complete shipping details without manual typing.
- **Do not require account creation before a user benefits from your App Clip.** Account setup is a complex process requiring time and effort. Consider making an account optional, or deferring the sign-up until after the user completes their task. If your App Clip requires an account to function, limit the information needed—for instance, by offering **Sign in with Apple**.
- **Provide a consistent, focused experience when transitioning to your app.** When users install the full application, it replaces the App Clip on their device. From that point forward, invocations intended for the App Clip will launch the full app instead. Ensure your application provides a focused, familiar experience to users who previously utilized the App Clip. Do not introduce additional steps that slow down this transition; for example, do not require users to log in again when moving from the App Clip to the app.
