# Legal requirements
App Clip Codes are only approved for use if they were generated using either App Store Connect or the App Clip Code Generator command-line tool, and if they adhere to these guidelines.

App Clip Codes are approved solely to indicate the availability of an App Clip. Apple reserves the right to update the App Clip Code design periodically at its sole discretion.

If your App Clip becomes inactive, you must also discontinue displaying the associated App Clip Code.

You may not incorporate the App Clip Code (including, but not limited to, the Apple Logo, the App Clip mark, or the App Clip Code designs) into your company name or product name. Furthermore, you may not seek copyright or trademark registration for the App Clip Codes or any of their components.

The App Clip Codes detailed in these guidelines must not be used in any way that could diminish, damage, or reduce the goodwill, value, or reputation associated with Apple or App Clips; infringe upon or violate the proprietary rights or trademarks of any third party; or mislead consumers regarding the source of products or services.

Apple retains all intellectual property rights, including trademarks and copyrights, pertaining to the materials provided for use hereunder, which includes, without limitation, the App Clip Codes and their constituent elements.

Do not add any symbols to App Clip Codes created via App Store Connect or the App Clip Code Generator command-line tool.

Do not translate any Apple trademark. All Apple trademarks must remain in English, even when appearing within text written in a language other than English. Translations of the legal notice and credit lines (but not the trademarks) may be used in materials distributed outside the U.S., provided Apple grants approval.

For further details regarding the use of Apple trademarks, consult [Guidelines for Using Apple Trademarks](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/guidelinesfor3rdparties.html).
