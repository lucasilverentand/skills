# App Clips — overview
Users encounter and initiate App Clips across many scenarios and contexts. When physically present, users can launch an App Clip by scanning an App Clip Code, NFC tag, or QR code. The App Clip Code is often the most effective method for discovery and launching because its unique design is instantly recognizable, assuring users of a fast and secure entry point.

On their device, users can launch an App Clip through location-based suggestions enabled in Siri Suggestions, the Maps application, Smart App Banners on websites, App Clip cards in Safari, or by tapping shared links within the Messages app. Starting with iOS 17, an application can include links and App Clip previews that allow users to launch another app's App Clip.

Consider developing an App Clip if your application provides a transient, in-the-moment experience that enables users to complete a task within a defined timeframe. For instance:

- A rental bicycle service could include an App Clip Code that users scan or tap to launch an App Clip allowing them to rent the bike.
- A coffee shop could offer an App Clip for quick advance orders, accessible via a Smart App Banner or an App Clip card on the shop's website. Customers can share a link to the website from Messages, which recipients then tap to launch the App Clip within the Messages app.
- A food truck could create marketing materials (such as a poster promoting a seasonal item) that include an App Clip Code. Users can scan this code using the device's Camera app to instantly launch the App Clip and place an order for the seasonal dish.
- A restaurant could enable diners to pay for their meal by launching an App Clip from the Maps app, a Siri Suggestion, or by holding their device near an App Clip Code or NFC tag at the table.
- A museum could allow visitors to scan App Clip Codes or QR codes on labels next to exhibits to launch an App Clip that provides audio commentary or reveals augmented reality content.

Consider using an App Clip to allow users to try your app or game before committing to a purchase or subscription. Focus on giving users the chance to experience and understand your application or game. For example:

- A game might offer an App Clip that provides a demo version, including a tutorial and the first level.
- A fitness application might provide an App Clip featuring a complimentary workout or guided meditation.
- A text editor could enable users to create and save a document using the demo App Clip.

For further developer guidance, please refer to [App Clips](apple:AppClip).
