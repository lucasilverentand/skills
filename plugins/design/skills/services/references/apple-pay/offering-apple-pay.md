# Offering Apple Pay
- **Offer Apple Pay on all devices and browsers that support it.** If a device lacks support for Apple Pay, do not present it as an available payment option. Utilize the Apple Pay APIs to determine device compatibility. For developer resources, refer to [PKPaymentAuthorizationController](apple:PassKit/PKPaymentAuthorizationController) (iOS, watchOS) and [canMakePayments](apple:ApplePayontheWeb/ApplePaySession/canMakePayments) (web).
- **If you utilize Apple Pay APIs to verify if a user has an active card in Wallet, you must designate Apple Pay as the primary payment method—though not exclusively so—in every location where those APIs are used.** For instance, you may pre-select Apple Pay when displaying it alongside alternative payment choices. See [Offering Apple Pay in Your App](apple:PassKit/offering-apple-pay-in-your-app) (iOS, watchOS) and [Checking for Apple Pay availability](apple:ApplePayontheWeb/checking-for-apple-pay-availability) (web) for developer guidance.
- **If you also provide alternative payment methods, offer Apple Pay concurrently.** Ensure that Apple Pay is featured with equal prominence to other options on every page or screen where payment methods are presented or accepted.
- **If you use an Apple Pay button to initiate the payment process, you must display it using the Apple-provided API.** Unlike a simple graphic button, buttons generated by the API maintain correct appearance and automatic localization.
- **If you use a custom button to begin the Apple Pay payment process, ensure your custom button does not display “Apple Pay” or the Apple Pay logo.** In this scenario, you must inform users that you accept Apple Pay by displaying the Apple Pay mark or referencing Apple Pay in text on the same page as your payment button. **Use Apple Pay buttons exclusively to initiate the Apple Pay transaction and, when appropriate, the Apple Pay setup process.** When a user selects an Apple Pay button to purchase but has not yet set up Apple Pay on their device, they are given the chance to complete the setup. Do not use Apple Pay buttons for any other purpose.
- **Do not hide an Apple Pay button or make it appear unusable.** If an Apple Pay button cannot be used immediately—for example, because a product size or color has not been chosen—you must gracefully address the issue after the user taps or clicks the button.
- **Use the Apple Pay mark solely to indicate acceptance of Apple Pay.** The Apple Pay mark itself does not facilitate payment. Never deploy it as a payment button or present it as one. If you use the Apple Pay mark to signify that Apple Pay is the selected payment method, you may create a separate custom button conforming to your application’s design to start the Apple Pay payment.
- **Inform search engines that Apple Pay is accepted on your website.** If your site employs semantic markup to provide product details to search engines, list Apple Pay as an accepted payment option.

For app developer guidance, see [Apple Pay](apple:PassKit/apple-pay). For website developer guidance, including how to determine the availability of Apple Pay on the web, see [Apple Pay on the Web](apple:ApplePayontheWeb).
