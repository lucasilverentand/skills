# Customizing the payment sheet
- **Only present and request essential information.** The payment sheet should avoid extraneous details that could confuse users or raise privacy concerns. For instance, if the purchase is an electronic gift card, a contact email address may be appropriate, but a shipping address should not be included. Displaying or requesting a physical delivery address in this scenario might incorrectly imply physical shipment.
- **Display the active coupon or promotional code, or provide a mechanism for users to enter it.** If customers can input a code before the payment sheet appears, displaying it on the sheet confirms its validity. Alternatively, allowing code entry within the payment sheet is especially useful during an express checkout process.
- **Allow users to select the shipping method within the payment sheet.** If space allows, clearly present a description, cost, and optionally, an estimated delivery or pickup date/range for every available option. In iOS 15 and later, leverage the shipping method’s calendar and time-zone support to ensure accurate delivery or pickup information, regardless of the customer's location. Refer to [PKDateComponentsRange](apple:PassKit/PKDateComponentsRange) for developer guidance.
- **For in-store pickup, consider allowing users to select a suitable pickup window.** You can use the shipping method details to provide a range of dates and times from which users can choose.
- **Use line items to detail additional charges, discounts, pending costs, add-on donations, recurring, and future payments.** A line item consists of a label and cost; if the payment is recurring, the line item can also specify its frequency. Do not use line items to list all individual products comprising the purchase. See [paymentSummaryItems](apple:PassKit/PKPaymentRequest/paymentSummaryItems) for developer guidance, and [Supporting donations](#Supporting-donations) for donation specifics.
- **Keep line items brief.** Ensure line items are specific and immediately understandable. Whenever possible, fit them onto a single line.

**Include the business name after the word *Pay* on the same line as the total.** Use the exact business name that customers will see when checking their bank or credit card statements. This assures users that the payment is going to the correct entity. For example: Pay [*Business_Name*].

- **If you are not the final merchant, specify both your business name and the end merchant’s name in the payment sheet.** There are different scenarios where your app, App Clip, or website facilitates a purchase from an end merchant unrelated to your company. For example, a marketplace app might enable purchases from an unfamiliar end merchant. Another case is an application offering self-checkout service for paying at an end merchant’s physical location without using their checkout counter. In these situations, users may not realize two businesses are involved, making it vital to name both entities and clarify their roles. When your app acts as an intermediary for an end merchant, clearly and concisely describe the situation in the Pay line using a format like Pay [*End_Merchant_Business_Name* (via *Your_Business_Name*)].
- **Clearly disclose when extra costs may arise after payment authorization.** In certain cases, the final cost might not be known at checkout. For instance, a ride service price based on distance or time may change post-checkout, or a customer might add a tip after product delivery. In such situations, and where local regulations permit, provide a clear explanation in the payment sheet alongside a subtotal marked as `Amount Pending`. If you are preauthorizing a specific amount, ensure the payment sheet accurately reflects this detail.
- **Handle data entry and payment errors gracefully.** If an error occurs during checkout, assist users in resolving it quickly so they can finalize the transaction. For related advice, see [Data validation](#Data-validation).
