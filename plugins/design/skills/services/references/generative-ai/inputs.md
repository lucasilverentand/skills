# Inputs
- **Educate users on how to utilize your generative feature.** To guide users and help them achieve optimal results, consider providing varied, predefined example inputs that illustrate the feature's capabilities.
- **Increase awareness of and mitigate hallucinations.** When a generative model generates content without sufficient grounding, it may produce plausible but fabricated information. These hallucinations carry the risk of misinforming users because the model can present inaccurate details—such as dates or biographical facts—as if they are factual. It is crucial to clearly communicate that AI-generated content may contain errors. You can minimize the occurrence and impact of hallucinations by carefully defining the scope of generation requests. Do not request factual information unless you are certain the model has access to verified and current data for that task. Furthermore, avoid deploying AI-generated content in scenarios where a potential hallucination could lead to misinformed or harmful outcomes.
- **Assess consequences and obtain consent before performing destructive or high-impact tasks.** Before executing any task, evaluate whether an error or the inability to reverse the action could impose undue burden or stress on users. Avoid automating destructive operations, such as deleting media, or actions that are difficult to undo, like making a purchase on behalf of another person. Generally, confirmation must be sought before performing any significant action on a user's behalf. Remember that specific situations may have prohibitions or additional constraints. You must review and comply with model-specific usage policies, as well as relevant government and regulatory AI guidelines applicable to every locale where the generative features are available.
