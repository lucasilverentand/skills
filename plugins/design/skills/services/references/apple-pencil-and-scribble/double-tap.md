# Double tap
- **Respect people’s settings for the double-tap gesture when they make sense in your app.** By default, Apple Pencil models that support this gesture toggle between the active tool and the eraser. However, users may configure it to switch between the current and previous tool, reveal/hide the color picker, or disable its function entirely. If your application supports these behaviors, allow users to utilize their preferred gesture settings. Should the system-wide double-tap options not apply to your app, you may still employ the gesture to alter the interaction mode. For instance, a 3D application featuring mesh-editing tools could use double tap to switch between the tool’s raise and lower states.
- **Give people a way to specify custom double-tap behavior if necessary.** If your app offers unique double-tap actions alongside or instead of the default behaviors, you must provide a control allowing users to select this custom mode. Users need clear awareness of which mode they are operating in; otherwise, confusion may arise if your application reacts differently to their inputs. In such cases, ensure that the custom behavior supported by your app is easily discoverable, but do not enable it by default.
- **Avoid using the double-tap gesture to perform an action that modifies content.** Since accidental double taps are possible, users may not realize your app has executed an action. Therefore, it is preferable to use double tap for actions that are easily reversible. Specifically, refrain from using double tap for any potentially destructive action that could lead to data loss.
