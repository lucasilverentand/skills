# Maintaining a shared context
When your shared activity runs in a Full Space, the system assists your application in maintaining a unified context by utilizing a single coordinate system to arrange both its content and all participants, automatically synchronizing the size, placement, and orientation of your app for every user. Your responsibility is to display objects, play sounds, and support interactions in ways that enhance the feeling of a shared experience.

- **Ensure all participants view the identical state of your application.** If your app supports multiple states—for instance, a media application offering both minimal and theater modes—you must prevent different participants from viewing disparate states, as this can undermine the sense of togetherness in a shared environment. The sole exception is when an individual needs to temporarily leave the shared activity; refer to [Adjusting a shared context](#Adjusting-a-shared-context) for guidance.
- **Leverage Spatial Audio to enhance your shared activity.** Incorporating Spatial Audio can help reinforce the realism of the shared experience. For details, consult **Playing audio**.
- **When feasible, allow users to resolve misunderstandings or conflicts that arise during a shared experience through natural, social interactions.** For example, if only one participant can operate a virtual tool at any given time, refrain from displaying UI elements like notifications or controls for tool usage; instead, allow participants to use speech or gestures to signal their intent to the group. If conflicts may occur during your shared activity—such as multiple users attempting to modify the same content simultaneously—consider implementing a straightforward protocol, such as "last change wins," allowing users to adopt this rule to define group-acceptable behavior.
- **Assist users in keeping their private and shared content clearly separated.** By default, the system distinctly differentiates a shared window from those that are not. For instance, when individuals use Music to listen together, the shared Music window appears as a new entity for everyone, while any individual's open library remains separate and unshared. If your app supports multiple windows, help users identify and select the one they wish to share, making it easy for them to distinguish between shared and unshared windows. If possible, also enable users to drag content they wish to share from a private window into the shared one.
