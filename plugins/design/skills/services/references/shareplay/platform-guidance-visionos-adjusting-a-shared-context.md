# Adjusting a shared context
It is sometimes beneficial to allow participants to customize their experience within a shared activity, such as adjusting settings for comfort or accessibility. Conversely, strictly maintaining a unified context might detract from the enjoyment of the experience. For instance, if content is optimized for a single viewing angle, each participant may require their own unique perspective.

- **Allow users to personalize their experience without altering the shared experience for others.** For example, participants may need to adjust different settings, such as volume or subtitles, to ensure accessibility or personal comfort during viewing and interaction.
- **Determine when each participant should have a unique view of the shared content.** Certain content is viewed most effectively from a specific viewpoint. For example, users can share a Spatial Capture in a standard window while other participants view their spatial Personas around it. However, achieving full depth perception in a Spatial Capture requires each individual to view it from the correct angle. In this scenario, a user could temporarily switch to a Full Space that conceals other participants and guarantees the proper viewing angle for them, even if everyone else remains in the standard window view. If providing personalized versions of your shared content is appropriate, ensure you continue synchronizing positions and app context to maintain the collective experience.
- **Ensure users can easily leave and rejoin a shared activity.** Occasionally, participants may need to handle an unrelated task within your application or engage with their physical environment. In such cases, you must provide a control or component that allows them to quickly return to the shared activity. Additionally, consider keeping the shared content visible so users remain informed about the ongoing experience while they are temporarily hidden in their spatial Persona.
