# Platform guidance — watchOS
The sign-up screen within your watchOS application must present the same information regarding subscription choices as found in other versions of your app. For a comprehensive list of required elements, refer to [Making signup effortless](#Making-signup-effortless). The following guidelines will assist you in designing a sign-up screen that feels appropriate for the Apple Watch environment.

- **Clearly articulate the functional differences between your app versions running on different devices.** If your watchOS application offers a subset of content or different functionality compared to other devices, you must clarify these distinctions. Be direct about the advantages of accessing subscription content via watchOS without suggesting that the experience is identical to other versions.
- **Consider utilizing a modal sheet to display the necessary information.** Once users respond to your call to action regarding subscription offers, you can use a modal sheet to present all required items in one view. Although users may need to scroll through the content, using a modal sheet keeps your app UI streamlined and concise. Furthermore, the default Close button on a modal sheet allows users to return to your free content with a single tap. If you choose a custom sign-up view instead of a modal sheet, ensure the flow is complete and efficient, including a Close or Cancel button that allows users to return to your free content.
- **Ensure subscription options are easily comparable on a small display.** Users must grasp the terms of each option before making a selection. Aim to present the duration and discount details for every option in a compact format that is quick to scan and compare. You have two ways to present subscription options in your watchOS app:
- Display each option using a separate button. Using one button per payment choice allows users to begin the signup process with a single tap. In this design, it is critical to group each button with its description so users understand the relationship between these elements, especially while scrolling.
- Display a list of options, followed by a button to initiate the signup process. Using a list format with one option per row provides a compact design that minimizes scrolling while keeping subscription choices easy to scan and comprehend. In this setup, the button's title can be updated to reflect the selected option.
