# Liquid Glass color
By default, **Liquid Glass** is inherently colorless; it adopts hues from the content displayed behind it. Color can be applied to certain Liquid Glass components, allowing them to mimic stained or colored glass. This technique is valuable for drawing attention to a specific control, such as a primary call to action, and it is the method employed by the system for prominent button styling. Symbols or text labels on Liquid Glass controls can also be colored.

For smaller components like tab bars and toolbars, the system dynamically adjusts Liquid Glass between light and dark appearances based on the underlying content. By default, symbols and text on these elements adhere to a monochromatic scheme: they darken when the background content is light, and lighten when it is dark. Larger elements, such as sidebars, appear more opaque to maintain legibility against complex backgrounds and accommodate richer content on the material's surface.

- **Apply color sparingly to the Liquid Glass material, and to symbols or text on the material.** If you introduce color, reserve it only for elements that genuinely benefit from emphasis, such as primary actions or status indicators. To highlight a primary action, apply the color to the background rather than to the symbols or text. For instance, prominent buttons like the Done button use the system's app accent color on the background to draw attention and enhance visual prominence. Do not apply color to the backgrounds of multiple controls. **Avoid using similar colors in control labels if your app has a colorful background.** While color can enhance visual appeal, reflect brand identity, or add playfulness, excessive color can be overwhelming and impair the readability of control labels. If your application features visually rich content or colorful backgrounds, favor a monochromatic appearance for tab bars and toolbars, or select an accent color that provides sufficient visual contrast. Conversely, in apps with primarily monochromatic content or backgrounds, using your brand color as the app accent can be an effective way to tailor the user experience and reflect your company's identity.
- **Be aware of the placement of color in the content layer.** Ensure your interface maintains adequate contrast by minimizing instances where similar colors overlap between the content layer and controls. Even if colorful content scrolls beneath the controls, ensure that its default or resting state—such as the top of a scrollable screen—remains clearly legible.
