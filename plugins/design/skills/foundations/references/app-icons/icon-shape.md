# Icon shape
An app icon’s geometry must align with a platform's visual language. In iOS, iPadOS, and macOS, icons are based on a square shape; the system then applies masking to achieve rounded corners that match the curvature of other interface elements and the physical device bezel. For tvOS, icons are rectangular with concentric edges. In visionOS and watchOS, icons are square, requiring circular masking by the system.

- **Produce appropriately shaped, unmasked layers.** The final icon shape is generated when the system masks all layer edges. For iOS, iPadOS, and macOS icons, supply square layers for the system to apply rounded corners. For visionOS and watchOS, provide square layers so the circular icon form can be created. For tvOS, supply rectangular layers for the system to apply rounded corners. Supplying layers with pre-applied masks degrades specular highlight effects and results in jagged edges.
- **Keep primary content centered to prevent truncation when the system adjusts corners or applies masking.** Pay close attention to proper content alignment within visionOS and watchOS icons. To aid in icon placement, utilize the grids found in the app icon production templates available at [Apple Design Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/).
