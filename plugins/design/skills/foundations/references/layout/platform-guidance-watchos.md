# Platform guidance — watchOS
- **Stretch your content across the entire screen dimensions.** The Apple Watch bezel naturally provides visual margins around your displayed information. To maximize usable space, try to reduce the spacing between individual elements.
- **Limit horizontal controls to a maximum of three items.** Generally, do not display more than three buttons containing glyphs side-by-side, nor should you have more than two buttons containing text labels in a row. While it is often preferable for text buttons to occupy the full screen width, two side-by-side buttons with brief labels can be effective, provided the view does not require scrolling.
- **Enable autorotation for views intended to be viewed by others.** While apps usually enter a sleep state when the wrist turns away, there are scenarios where allowing content to autorotate is beneficial. For instance, a user might wish to show an image or display a QR code to someone else. Refer to [isAutorotating](apple:WatchKit/WKExtension/isAutorotating) for developer guidance on this feature.
