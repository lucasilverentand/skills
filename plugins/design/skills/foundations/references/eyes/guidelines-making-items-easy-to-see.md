# Making items easy to see
- **Minimize visual distractions.** High levels of visual noise complicate users' ability to locate specific elements. Visual motion is even more distracting: when movement is detected—particularly in the periphery—users automatically track it, making sustained focus on the intended object difficult. For instance, revealing content near a button that users are currently viewing can cause them to involuntarily shift attention to the new content rather than maintaining focus on the button.
- **Make it easy for people to look at an item by providing enough space around it.** Since eyes naturally make minor, rapid directional adjustments even while focused on a single point, grouping UI elements too closely makes it challenging for users to view one without jumping between items. To ensure sufficient separation between interactive components, apply a margin of at least 16 points around each item's boundaries or position items so their centers are separated by a minimum of 60 points. For further guidance on arrangement, consult **Layout** and **Spatial layout**.
- **Avoid using a repeating pattern or texture that fills the field of view.** In certain instances, users' eyes may fixate on individual elements within a pattern or texture, leading to the perception that those elements possess varying depths. To prevent this effect, consider applying the pattern within a confined area.
