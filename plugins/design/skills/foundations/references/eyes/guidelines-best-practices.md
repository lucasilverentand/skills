# Best practices
- **Always give people multiple ways to interact with your app.** Design your application to accommodate the accessibility features users employ to customize how they engage with their devices. For detailed guidance, consult **Accessibility**.
- **Design for visual comfort.** Ensure users can achieve their primary goal by keeping necessary objects within their **Field of view**. When running in Shared Space or a Full Space, the system automatically positions the initial window or volume opened by users in a convenient location. Furthermore, while operating in a Full Space, your application or game may request head pose information to assist with appropriate 3D content placement. Regardless of the scenario, visual comfort is enhanced by preventing users from needing to make rapid eye adjustments across a large area or through multiple planes of depth. See **Depth** for more information.
- **Place content at a comfortable viewing distance.** For instance, to maintain user comfort during extended reading or engagement, aim to position content at least one meter away. In general, placing content very close to users should be reserved only for interactions or views that are momentary.
- **Prefer using standard UI components.** System-provided elements respond predictably when viewed by users. If your custom components utilize unique visual indicators for feedback, it may complicate the user's ability to learn and recall how those components operate.
