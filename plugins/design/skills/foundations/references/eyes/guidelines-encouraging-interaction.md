# Encouraging interaction
- **Consider using subtle visual cues to encourage people to look at the item they’re most likely to want.** For instance, positioning the item near the center of the field of view or employing techniques such as gentle movement, heightened contrast, or variations in hue or size can be effective in capturing attention. Generally, cues should be noticeable without appearing overly dramatic or aggressive.
- **In general, give an interactive item a rounded shape.** Since human vision naturally tends toward corners, maintaining focus on the center of a sharp-angled shape can be challenging. A higher degree of curvature in an item's form makes it easier for users to track with their eyes. **If you create an interactive component that consists of more than one element, be sure to provide an overall containing shape that visionOS can highlight.** For example, if a label and an image work together as a single interactive unit, you must define a custom region that includes both elements. This allows visionOS to highlight the entire area when users focus on either individual part.
