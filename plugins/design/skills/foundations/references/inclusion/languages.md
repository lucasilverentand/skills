# Languages
Users expect to tailor their device experience by selecting a language for text and a region for formatting values such as dates, times, and currency. To accommodate a global audience, you must first prepare your software to handle languages and regions beyond your own—a process known as *internationalization*—and subsequently provide translated text and resources for specific locales. For a comprehensive guide on internationalization, consult [Expanding your app to new markets](https://developer.apple.com/localization/); for developer instructions on localization, refer to [Localization](apple:Xcode/localization).

Fostering an inclusive experience also aids in preparing for localization. For instance, employing plain language, avoiding gender-specific references, representing diverse populations, and omitting stereotypes or culture-bound content positions your application well for subsequent translation into different languages. Furthermore, utilizing **SF Symbols** for glyphs within your app or game can streamline the localization process. In addition to offering numerous language-specific glyphs, SF Symbols includes symbols usable in both left-to-right and right-to-left contexts; guidance on this is available at **Right to left**.

As you localize your application or game and its associated content, remain mindful of how color is utilized. Since colors frequently carry deep culture-specific meanings, it is crucial to understand how different locales perceive specific hues. For example, white may symbolize death or mourning in some cultures while representing purity or peace in others. If you use color to convey meaning, ensure that your chosen colors communicate the identical message across every localized version of your software.
