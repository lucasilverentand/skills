# Gender identity
Throughout history, different cultures have acknowledged a wide spectrum of self-identity and expression that extends beyond the traditional binary definitions of man and woman.

To ensure your app or game is welcoming to all users, avoid making unnecessary references to specific genders. For instance, instead of using copy like, “You can let a subscriber post his or her recipes to your shared folder,” which contains gendered language, you could use the alternative: “Subscribers can post recipes to your shared folder.” This revised phrasing achieves inclusivity by using the gender-neutral noun “subscribers” and eliminating the need for singular, gendered pronouns like “his” or “her,” which aids localization into languages that rely on gendered pronouns.

Furthermore, you can often bypass referencing a specific gender when designing avatars, emojis, glyphs, or game characters. To welcome everyone to your application or game, it is best practice to provide users with the tools necessary for them to customize these elements as they wish.

If you need to represent a generic individual or group, utilize nongendered human imagery. This reinforces the message that *generic person* refers to any *human*, not exclusively a *man* or *woman*.
