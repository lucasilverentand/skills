# Inclusive by design
Well-designed applications and games are built upon simple, intuitive user experiences. To achieve an intuitive design, you must begin by researching users' goals and viewpoints so that the content presented resonates with them.

Empathy is a crucial tool in this investigation because it allows you to understand how individuals with varying perspectives might react to the content and experiences you develop. For instance, you may find that from certain viewpoints, a word or image is unclear or carries an unintended meaning.

While every individual's perspective is a unique and dynamic combination of human qualities, all perspectives stem from shared human characteristics and experiences, including:

- Age
- Gender and gender identity
- Race and ethnicity
- Sexuality
- Physical attributes
- Cognitive attributes
- Permanent, temporary, and situational disabilities
- Language and culture
- Religion
- Education
- Political or philosophical opinions
- Social and economic context

When evaluating your app or game through these diverse lenses, do not frame the task solely as searching for content that might cause offense. Although no design should contain offensive material or experiences, being inoffensive does not automatically equate to being inclusive. Focusing on inclusion helps you proactively avoid potentially offensive content while simultaneously creating a welcoming experience enjoyable for everyone.
