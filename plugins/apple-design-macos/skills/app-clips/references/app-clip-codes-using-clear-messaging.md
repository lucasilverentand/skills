# Using clear messaging
Ensure that clear instructions are provided on how users can utilize the App Clip Code to launch your App Clip, particularly if you deploy a design without the official App Clip logo. When displaying an App Clip Code in materials like emails or posters, include a call to action using either the suggested phrasing or your own copy. The call to action must always be straightforward and unambiguous.

For a scan-only App Clip Code, you may use the following prompts:

- Scan to [*describe what users can accomplish with your App Clip*].
- Use the camera on your iPhone or iPad to scan and [describe what users can accomplish with your App Clip].

For an NFC-integrated App Clip Code, you may use the following prompts:

- Scan to [*describe what users can accomplish with your App Clip*].
- Place your iPhone near the [*object name*] to launch an App Clip that allows you to [*describe what a person can accomplish with your App Clip*].

For further details, consult [NFC](nfc.md).

**When referencing your App Clip and App Clip Codes, you must comply with [Guidelines for Using Apple Trademarks](https://www.apple.com/legal/intellectual-property/guidelinesfor3rdparties.html).** For instance, Apple trademarks must not appear in your app name or images; always use title case when referring to App Clips or App Clip Code. Refer to [Legal requirements](#Legal-requirements) for additional information.
