# Symbols and branding
CareKit employs different integrated icons to assist users in understanding app functionalities. For instance, CareKit may present phone, messaging, and envelope icons within a contact screen, or the clock icon in a task log.

While you have the option to modify default symbols, most view formats function optimally when utilizing the icons provided by CareKit. The exception is the highly configurable grid-style task view, which allows you to display your bespoke UI elements in a grid arrangement.

In a grid layout, you may wish to feature custom symbols that align with the unique content and experience of your application. You could utilize symbols to denote task groupings; for instance, a pill icon for medication tasks or a walking person icon for exercise activities. In such instances, consider leveraging [SF Symbols](sf-symbols.md) to visually represent custom items within your app.

Integrating SF Symbols into your application provides you with:

- Designs that harmonize with CareKit’s established visual identity
- The capability to develop custom symbols representing your app's unique content
- **Design a symbol relevant to care.** If customization is required, ensure the design maintains a close connection to your application or the broader concept of health and wellness. Avoid creating symbols that are purely decorative or using a corporate logo as a custom indicator.
- **Incorporate subtle and refined branding.** Users utilize CareKit applications to pursue their health and wellness objectives; they should not encounter advertising. To prevent distractions from the care plan, integrate your brand subtly through your app's color palette and communication style.
