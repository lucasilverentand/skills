# Designing for games
When users play your game on an Apple device, they experience the world you created while utilizing the platform features they are familiar with

## When to use
- User asks about **designing for games** on macOS (e.g. `"how do I design designing for games for Mac"`).
- User is building a Mac UI that needs designing for games and wants to follow Apple's guidelines.
- User asks to audit or review designing for games in a macOS design.
- User mentions designing for games in the context of a Mac app, game, or interface.

## Quick principles
- **Enable gameplay immediately upon installation completion** — Players should not face delays between installing your game and experiencing it
- **Supply excellent default configurations** — Users appreciate being able to begin playing without needing extensive initial setup
- **Educate players through action** — Players often grasp new mechanics and information more effectively when they encounter them within the context of your game's environment
- **Postpone requests until appropriate timing** — You must avoid overwhelming players with numerous requests before they begin playing
- **Ensure text is always legible** — If game text is difficult to read, players may struggle to follow the narrative, grasp critical instructions or information, and remain engaged…
- **Ensure buttons are always easy to use** — Buttons that are either too small or placed too closely together can frustrate players and detract from the enjoyment of gameplay
- **Prefer resolution-independent textures and graphics** — If creating assets that are independent of resolution is not feasible, match your game's rendering resolution to the device's native resolution
- **Integrate device features into your layout** — A device's physical characteristics, such as rounded corners or a camera housing, can influence parts of your interface
- **Ensure in-game menus adapt to different aspect ratios** — Games must render correctly and function properly across different aspect ratios, including 16:10, 19.5:9, and 4:3
- **Design for the full-screen experience** — Players often prefer playing a game in an immersive, distraction-free full-screen environment
- **Support each platform’s default interaction method** — For instance, mobile games are typically played using touch on iPhone; Mac players usually expect keyboard, mouse, or trackpad support; and visionOS…
- **Support physical game controllers, while also giving people alternatives** — All platforms except watchOS support dedicated game controllers
