# Explicit feedback
Explicit feedback offers users actionable intelligence that your application can utilize to enhance the content and overall experience presented to them. Unlike [implicit](#Implicit-feedback) information, which your app infers from user behavior, explicit feedback is data users supply in direct response to a specific prompt from the app.

*Favoriting*, which marks an item for future quick retrieval, and *social feedback*, which conveys emotions regarding others, are common interactions that might appear to be mechanisms for explicit feedback. However, these tools actually yield implicit feedback because they do not support app-specific requests. Users employ favoriting and social feedback to meet their own objectives, allowing the app to gather implicit data from these actions.

- **Only request explicit feedback when it is truly necessary.** Since users must take action to provide explicit feedback, it is preferable to avoid requesting it unless required. Instead, consider leveraging implicit feedback to understand how users interact with your app without requiring them to perform extra tasks.
- **Always ensure providing explicit feedback is a voluntary action.** You need to convey that while explicit feedback can aid in improving the experience, it is not mandatory for users.
- **Use clear, straightforward language to describe each explicit feedback option and its resulting consequences.** Avoid vague terms like *dislike*, as such words fail to communicate consequences and can be difficult to translate. Instead, describe each choice in a manner that helps users understand the outcome of their selection, such as:
- Suggest less pop music
- Suggest more thrillers
- Mute politics for a week
- **Include icons in an option description if they aid user understanding.** Icons can help clarify or emphasize a part of the option description. However, do not rely solely on an icon, as it may lack the clarity needed to convey granularity or consequences.
- **Consider offering multiple choices when soliciting explicit feedback.** Providing [multiple options](#Multiple-options) grants users a sense of control and assists them in identifying unwanted suggestions or removing them from your app. To facilitate feedback provision, consider offering options that become progressively more detailed, making it easier for users to refine their response.
- **React immediately to explicit feedback and implement the resulting changes.** For instance, if users indicate they do not wish to see certain content, that content should instantly vanish from their view and cease appearing elsewhere in your application. When you respond promptly to feedback and demonstrate that your app has registered it, you build user trust in the value of providing input.
- **Consider using explicit feedback to help refine when and where results are displayed.** For example, users might appreciate a result but wish to view it only at specific times or in certain contexts. Explicit feedback regarding the timing and location of result displays can help you fine-tune the user experience within your app.
