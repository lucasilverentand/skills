# Platform guidance — visionOS — overview
Widgets in visionOS are three-dimensional objects users place on a horizontal or vertical surface. When placed, the widget maintains its location even if the user powers Apple Vision Pro off and back on. Widgets are rendered at a consistent, real-world scale. Their perceived presence is influenced by their size, *mounting style*, and *treatment style*.

visionOS widgets display in full color by default. However, they adopt an accented rendering mode when users apply tint colors using system-provided palettes. Furthermore, widgets utilizing the elevated mounting style allow customization of frame width, along with other widget-specific options. For instance, while visionOS does not offer system-wide light or dark appearances, the Music poster widget provides its own customization allowing users to select a light or dark theme generated from the displayed album art.

For guidance intended for developers, refer to [Updating your widgets for visionOS](apple:WidgetKit/Updating-your-widgets-for-visionOS).

- **Adapt your design and content for the spatial experience Apple Vision Pro provides.** In visionOS, widgets are not isolated elements; they exist within environments such as living rooms, kitchens, and offices. When adapting existing widgets or designing new ones for visionOS, consider this context early on, treating the widget as part of a user's physical surroundings. For example, the Music widget can adopt a poster-like appearance suitable for being viewed across a room with large typography and high-resolution imagery, while a productivity application might offer a small widget optimized for placement on a desk.
- **Test your widgets across the full range of system color palettes and in different lighting conditions.** Ensure that the widget's tone, contrast, and legibility remain intentional and consistent. If you choose to prevent UI elements from being tinted, test your widget in every provided tint color palette to confirm that the untinted elements remain legible when a user customizes their widgets using tint colors.
