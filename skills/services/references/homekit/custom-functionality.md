# Custom functionality
- **Clearly define the app's capabilities and when users might need to utilize the Home app.** For instance, if your application only controls lights, consider encouraging users to build a comprehensive "Movie Time" scene that includes not just dimming the lights, but also closing shades and powering on a TV. To achieve this, initially guide users through setting up a scene using only your accessory's functions—in this case, dimming the lights. Subsequently, your app can suggest they open the Home app to incorporate their other HomeKit-compatible devices like shades and the TV into the scene you helped them create. For instructions on how to reference the Home app, consult [Referring to HomeKit](#Referring-to-HomeKit).
- **Defer control to HomeKit if your database schema differs from the HomeKit schema.** Provide a smooth user experience by automatically mirroring any modifications made within the Home app or other third-party HomeKit applications. If users must manage discrepancies within your application, present the conflict visually so they have a clear understanding of the choice required. For example, if a user modifies an accessory’s service name in the Home app, your application can detect this change and display both names side-by-side to confirm the user wishes to adopt the new name within your app as well.
- **Obtain explicit permission before updating the HomeKit database when users make changes in your app.** To prevent unexpected modifications within the Home app, it is crucial to secure permission or an indication of intent before writing data to the database. Specifically, never overwrite HomeKit database settings without the user's clear direction.

#### Cameras
Your application has the capability to display either still images or streaming video feeds from a linked HomeKit IP camera.

- **Do not obstruct the camera view.** While you may enhance the camera feed with helpful features, such as alerts drawing attention to noteworthy activity, ensure that no other content covers any part of the live image.
- **Only display a microphone button if the camera supports two-way audio.** A microphone control that does not function wastes screen real estate within your application and may mislead users.
