# Create shortcut titles and subtitles
Shortcut titles and subtitles are displayed when the system suggests them. On iPhone and Apple Watch, Siri Suggestions also allow a shortcut to feature an image.

- **Be concise but descriptive.** An effective title clearly communicates the action performed when the shortcut executes. A subtitle offers supplementary details without repeating the information presented in the title.
- **Start titles with a verb and use sentence-style capitalization without punctuation.** Treat a shortcut title as a brief command.

||Example title|
|---|---|
|✓|*Order my favorite coffee*|
|✗|*Large latte*|
|✓|*Show today’s forecast*|
|✗|*Weather forecast*|

- **Lead with important information.** Since long titles and subtitles may be shortened in certain contexts, depending on the device's screen size, prioritize essential details.
- **Exclude your app name.** The operating system already identifies the application linked to the shortcut.
- **Localize titles and subtitles.** Offering content across many languages guarantees an equally positive experience for users globally.
- **Consider providing a custom image for a more engaging suggestion.** For instance, the shortcut to *Order my favorite coffee* could use an image of the customer's preferred beverage. The required dimensions are:
- 60x60 pt (180x180 px @ 3x) for display within an iOS application
- 34x34 pt (68x68 px @2x) for display on the 44mm Apple Watch Siri face (watchOS scales down the image for smaller watches)
