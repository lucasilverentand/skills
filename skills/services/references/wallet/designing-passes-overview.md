# Designing passes — overview
- **Design a pass that looks great and works well on all devices.** Passes may appear differently across different devices. For instance, the display on Apple Watch differs from that on iPhone (refer to [Passes for Apple Watch](#Passes-for-Apple-Watch) for guidance). Do not include critical information in elements that may be unavailable on specific devices. Furthermore, do not add padding to images; for example, watchOS crops surrounding white space from certain visuals.
- **Avoid using device-specific language.** Since you cannot predict the device users will employ to view your pass, refrain from including text that might be ambiguous on a particular hardware. For example, instructions like "slide to view" are irrelevant when displayed on Apple Watch.
- **Make your pass instantly identifiable.** Utilizing color—especially a hue associated with your brand—can help users recognize your pass immediately upon viewing it. Ensure that the chosen background allows the pass content to remain comfortably legible.
- **Keep the front of a pass uncluttered so people can get important information at a glance.** Display essential details, such as an account balance or event date, in the upper-right section of the pass so they remain visible even when the pass is collapsed in Wallet. Use the remainder of the pass front for important information; consider placing supplementary details on the back of the pass (iOS) or within a dedicated details screen (watchOS).
- **Prefer an NFC-compatible pass.** Users value the convenience of a contactless pass, as it allows them to simply hold their device near a reader. If you support both NFC and a barcode or QR code, the respective code should appear on the back of the pass (in iOS) or in the details screen (in watchOS). In iOS, you may display a QR code or barcode on the front of your pass if necessary for your design.
- **Reduce image sizes for optimal performance.** Since passes are received via email or a webpage, use the smallest possible image files to ensure downloads occur as quickly as possible.
- **Provide an icon that represents your company or brand.** The system incorporates your icon when displaying information about a relevant pass on the Lock Screen. Mail also uses this icon to represent your pass within an email message. You may use either your app icon or design a dedicated icon for this purpose.
