# Creating content for an App Clip card
The App Clip card is the initial point of contact between users and your App Clip; therefore, carefully consider both its imagery and accompanying text.

- **Be informative.** Ensure the App Clip card image clearly conveys the features, supported tasks, or content provided by your App Clip.
- **Prefer photography and graphics.** Do not use a screenshot of your app’s user interface, as it may fail to communicate the App Clip's purpose. Instead, use an image that conveys the App Clip’s value proposition or a photograph of the associated business location or point of interest.
- **Avoid using text.** Text within the header image is not localizable, may be difficult to read, and can detract from the aesthetic quality of the card image.
- **Adhere to image requirements.** The image must be a PNG or JPEG file, sized at 1800x1200 px, and must not contain transparency.
- **Use concise copy.** Since an App Clip card includes both a title and a subtitle, clearly articulate the App Clip's function within the available space to ensure immediate understanding. The title must not exceed 30 characters, and the subtitle must not exceed 56 characters.
- **Pick a verb for the action button that best fits your App Clip.** The available verbs are *View*, *Play*, or *Open*. Select *View* if your App Clip delivers media or informational/educational content. Use *Play* for games. Select *Open* for all other types of App Clips.
