# Supporting offer codes
In iOS and iPadOS, subscription offer codes allow developers to utilize both online and offline channels to grant new, current, or lapsed subscribers free or discounted access to your subscription content. Examples include distributing codes via email, at a physical location, or printed on merchandise.

You must support two types of offer codes:

- A *one-time use code* is a unique identifier generated in App Store Connect. Users can redeem this code via a [redemption URL](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/manage-subscriptions/set-up-offer-codes/#distribute-offer-codes) (a shareable link), within your app (if you support redemption), or by entering it in the App Store, which prompts installation if the app hasn't been downloaded. Consider using one-time use codes when your distribution volume is low or when you need to restrict access.
- A *custom code* is a code defined by you, such as NEWYEAR or SPRINGSALE. Users can redeem this custom code through a redemption URL or within your app (if you support redemption). Consider using a custom code for large campaigns requiring mass distribution.

For developer guidance on implementing offer codes, refer to [Offer codes](apple:storekit/implementing-offer-codes-in-your-app) and [Set up offer codes](https://developer.apple.com/help/app-store-connect/manage-subscriptions/set-up-offer-codes). For guidance on other types of offers, see [Providing subscription offers](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/subscriptions/#providing-subscription-offers).

- **Clearly explain offer details.** Provide a clear and brief description of your offer in your marketing materials to assist users in making an informed choice.
- **Follow guidelines for creating a custom code.** A custom code must consist solely of alphanumeric ASCII characters. Do not include special characters, including those from Chinese or Arabic scripts.
- **Tell people how to redeem a custom code.** Since users cannot redeem a custom code by entering it in their App Store account settings, it is crucial to inform them that redemption must occur via a redemption URL or within your app.
- **Consider supporting offer redemption within your app.** The system automatically provides the necessary screens for the offer-redemption workflow, regardless of whether the user redeems in your app or the App Store. When you use the StoreKit API to enable in-app redemption, the only custom UI required is one that initiates the system-provided flow. For developer guidance, consult [presentOfferCodeRedeemSheet(in:)](apple:StoreKit/AppStore/presentOfferCodeRedeemSheet(in:)) and [offerCodeRedemption(isPresented:onCompletion:)](apple:SwiftUI/View/offerCodeRedemption(isPresented:onCompletion:)). There are several appropriate locations for this custom UI, such as a "Redeem Code" button on your paywall, onboarding screens, or within the app's settings.

After users tap your custom redeem button, the system automatically displays a series of code-redemption screens, similar to those shown below.

- **Supply an engaging and informative promotional image.** This optional image can help users understand the value proposition of your content. If no promotional image is provided, the code redemption screens default to using your app icon. Learn more at [Promoting your in-app purchases](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/promoting-in-app-purchases/).
- **Help people benefit from unlocked content as soon as they complete the redemption flow.** Consider how to align the post-redemption experience in your app with the subscriber's new status. For instance, you might offer a welcome sequence for new subscribers or a quick tour of new features for an existing subscriber gaining additional functionality. Specifically, be prepared to welcome users who subscribe before they first open your app. For example, if account creation or sign-in is required to use your app, make this process as seamless as possible for new subscribers encountering it for the first time.
