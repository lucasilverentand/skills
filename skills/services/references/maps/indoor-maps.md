# Indoor maps
Apps associated with specific locations, such as stadiums or shopping centers, can develop custom interactive maps to assist users in locating and navigating indoor points of interest. These indoor maps may include overlays that highlight particular zones, including rooms, kiosks, and other locations. They can also feature text labels, icons, and routing information.

- **Adjust map detail based on the zoom level.** Excessive detail can lead to a cluttered appearance. Display large areas, like buildings and rooms, at all zoom levels. Then, gradually introduce more granular features and labels as the map is magnified. For instance, an airport map might initially display only terminals and gates when zoomed out, but reveal individual stores and restrooms upon zooming in.
- **Use distinctive styling to differentiate the features of your map.** Employing color alongside icons helps distinguish different types of areas, services, and stores, allowing users to quickly identify what they seek.
- **Offer a floor picker if your venue includes multiple levels.** A floor picker allows users to rapidly switch between different floors. If you implement this feature, keep the floor identifiers brief for simplicity. In most scenarios, a list of floor numbers is sufficient rather than using floor names.
- **Include surrounding areas to provide context.** Adjacent streets, playgrounds, and nearby locations can all aid users in orienting themselves while using your map. If these external areas are noninteractive, use dimming and a distinct color palette to signify their supplemental nature.
- **Consider supporting navigation between your venue and nearby transit points.** Facilitate easy entry and exit from your location by offering routing to and from adjacent bus stops, train stations, parking garages, and other transit hubs. You may also provide a quick option for users to switch to Apple Maps for extended navigation capabilities.
- **Limit scrolling outside of your venue.** This prevents users from becoming disoriented when they swipe too far on the map. Whenever possible, ensure that a portion of your indoor map remains visible on screen at all times. To assist with orientation, you may need to calibrate the amount of scrolling permitted based on the current zoom level.
- **Design an indoor map that feels like a natural extension of your app.** Do not attempt to mimic the aesthetic of Apple Maps. Instead, ensure that area overlays, icons, and text align with your app's established visual style. For guidance, refer to [Indoor Mapping Data Format](https://register.apple.com/resources/imdf/).
