# Spatial layout — full guidelines — overview
A person’s *field of view* refers to the visual area they perceive without needing to move their head. When using Apple Vision Pro, the size of an individual's field of view depends on factors such as how they configure the Light Seal and their level of peripheral vision.

> **Important**
> The system does not supply data regarding a person's field of view.

- **Center important content within the field of view.** By default, visionOS launches an application directly ahead of users, positioning it within their field of view. In immersive scenarios, you can maintain user focus on critical information by keeping it centered and refraining from displaying rapid motion or overly bright, high-contrast elements in the periphery.
- **Avoid anchoring content to the wearer’s head.** While your application should generally remain within the field of view, rigidly anchoring content statically in front of a person can induce feelings of being trapped, restricted, or uneasy. This effect is amplified if the content blocks significant passthrough views or diminishes the perceived stability of their environment. Instead, anchor objects within the user's space, allowing them the freedom to scan naturally and view items from different locations.
